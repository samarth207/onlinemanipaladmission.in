    Semester 1

    Entrepreneurial Practice

    This subject explores identifying opportunities and building viable business ventures through strategic planning, innovation, and calculated risk-taking. Learners develop skills in market analysis, financial management, and adaptive leadership while learning to balance profitability with ethical practices and social responsibility.

    Business Communication (WAC)

    Learners develop essential communication skills for professional success across written, verbal, and digital formats. The course covers fundamental writing principles, tone and style, and cultural considerations, equipping students to communicate effectively with diverse audiences in today's interconnected business environment.

    Managerial Economics

    This course explores macroeconomic principles essential for managerial decision-making, covering economic cycles, fiscal and monetary policies, inflation, exchange rates, and balance of payments. Learners develop analytical skills to navigate complex economic environments and make informed strategic business decisions.

    Financial Accounting

    This course develops fundamental accounting skills including recording, adjusting, analyzing, and reporting financial information. Learners learn depreciation accounting and non-financial reporting practices essential for accurate financial documentation and analysis.

    Data Visualisation (Excel/Tableau)

    This course develops essential data visualization skills using Excel and Tableau, covering chart creation, PivotTables, interactive dashboards, and advanced formatting techniques. Learners get to transform raw data into compelling visual stories that communicate insights clearly and support effective business decision-making.

    Organizational Behaviour

    This course explores individual and group behavior in organizations, focusing on management, motivation, leadership, and communication. It also addresses modern challenges like diversity, stress, culture, innovation, and global management practices.

    Marketing Management

    This course introduces core marketing principles, including the marketing mix, pricing, promotion, and distribution strategies. It also explores e-marketing, CRM, international and ethical marketing through cases, preparing learners for service, retail, and digital sectors.
    Semester 2

    Business Research Methods (R/Python)

    This course introduces research methodologies and R programming for data collection, analysis, and statistical techniques. Learners get to turn data insights into actionable business strategies and clear research reports.

    Operation Management

    This course focuses on operations and supply chain management, including forecasting, capacity planning, materials, and quality systems. Learners build practical skills to analyze processes, optimize resources, resolve bottlenecks, and improve operational efficiency.

    Human Resource Management

    This course examines key HRM functions like recruitment, performance, compensation, and employee relations—aligned with organizational strategy. Learners also explore diversity, engagement, and legal issues to enhance organizational performance.

    Management Accounting

    This course covers cost analysis, budgeting, and performance measurement for strategic decision-making, including activity-based costing, balanced scorecards, and CVP analysis.

    Financial Management

    This course covers capital budgeting, financial planning, risk and cash flow management, and working capital optimization. Learners use case studies to analyze performance and develop strategies to maximize organizational value.

    Legal Aspects of Business

    This course covers essential legal frameworks governing businesses in India, including the Indian Contract Act, Companies Act 2013. Learners explore regulations and intellectual property to ensure legal compliance and ethical practice.

    Business Communication (VAC)

    This course builds verbal and digital communication skills for meetings, interviews, and group discussions. It emphasizes effective business communication using modern technology.
    Semester 3

    Core Subjects

    Strategic Management

    This course develops strategic thinking to achieve sustainable competitive advantage. Learners learn to align strategies with organizational goals, adapt to market changes, and navigate global business challenges.

    What are the eligibility criteria for an Online MBA in BFSI course? Who can apply?

    Educational qualification 

    Candidates must have a 10 + 2 + 3-year bachelor’s degree from a recognized university/institution, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities. 

    Grades 

    Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate in graduation (45% for reserved categories). 

    What is the duration of MBA in BFSI program?

    Like an on-campus MBA, the online MBA in BFSI is a two-year degree program consisting of four semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 4 years.

    What is the admission process for an online MBA BFSI degree?

    This program is ideal for graduates and professionals who want to make a career in the banking, insurance, asset management, or financial services industries. Most of all, it is beneficial for people who want to improve their skills or change their jobs to the BFSI sector.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credit. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national need to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email to info@muj.onlinemanipal.com for any queries.
