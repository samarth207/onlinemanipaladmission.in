Advantages of Our OnlineMBA Courses

UGC-entitled degrees

From NAAC accredited universities with decades of academic legacy in delivering quality education

Learn on Your Schedule

Having to log in and study from anywhere, at any time, is one of the most significant advantages of our online MBA programs. This provides learners with the pace to balance their education, work, family, and other commitments.

Career Advancement Without a Break

An online MBA is a valuable career enhancement plan, as it enables individuals to continue their employment without putting it on hold while still allowing them to gain a postgraduate qualification easily.

Cost-Effective and Time-Saving

In comparison with traditional MBAs, online MBAs are often more cost-effective. Additionally, students can save a significant amount by eliminating commuting costs and focusing entirely on their coursework.

Access to Global Learning and Networking

With an online MBA, learners are acquainted with fellow students and faculty from around the world. This builds learning about new cultures, fosters strong global networking, and also involves working on cross-cultural projects.

Same Degree, Same Value

Reputed universities offer online MBAs with the same curriculum and faculty as their on-campus programs. You earn the same accredited degree, which holds equal value in the job market.

Can I do an online MBA after 12th?

You can’t get an online MBA right after the 12th grade since you need a Bachelor’s degree first. You can, however, sign up for a 5-year Integrated BBA+MBA program. This dual-degree arrangement makes it easy for you to move from undergraduate to graduate studies without having to take a separate entrance exam. Many leading colleges provide this option to students who know what they want to do with their management degree. It saves time and money.

How to choose the right MBA specialization for your career?

Choosing the correct MBA concentration is a strategic choice that should take into account your work history and the needs of the job market in the future. Think about these three important pillars to help you make a smart choice: 

  • Think about what you’re good at and what you want to do. If you like looking at data and patterns, Business Analytics or Finance can be a good fit for you. On the other hand, if you are good at making connections and planning, Marketing or Human Resources are excellent jobs for you. 
  • Check out market trends by finding out which industries are rising. For example, there has been a huge increase in demand for Healthcare Management and Supply Chain recently. 
  • Career Goals and ROI: Decide if you want to move up in your present field or switch to a new one. An Online MBA in IT Management is great for people who work in tech and want to move up in their careers. A General Management MBA is the most flexible across all fields. 

Always check the curriculum of the specialization you choose to make sure it includes the most up-to-date digital tools and case studies that are relevant to your field.

How does online MBA offer more flexibility than regular MBA classes?

Online MBAs give you more freedom by letting you access course materials whenever you choose instead of having to go to class at set times. You can study from anywhere, at any time, because you don’t have to be at a certain place or time. You can do it over lunch or late at night. You don’t have to drive to work every day, and you can keep your full-time job, which means you can improve your career and your personal life at the same time.

Is online MBA valid in India?

An online MBA is valid in India as long as the University Grants Commission (UGC) and the Distance Education Bureau (DEB) acknowledge the program. Since 2020, the government has said that degrees achieved through authorized online platforms are the same as traditional on-campus degrees for careers and higher education. This validation makes sure that both top business recruiters and government employers around the country acknowledge your qualification.

How to do MBA online in India?

If you want to get an online MBA in India, do these things: 

  • Check Your Eligibility: You need to have a bachelor’s degree from an accredited university and at least 50% of the marks (45% for restricted categories). You don’t need to take the CAT or GMAT for most programs. 
  • Check Recognition: Make sure the university is UGC-DEB accredited for online degrees. 
  • Online Application: Go to the university’s official website, fill out the application, and upload your degree, ID proof, and photo. 

To get into the Learning Management System (LMS), you need to pay the semester or yearly fees.

What factors to consider before choosing an online MBA program?

  • Accreditation: Ensure the program is UGC-DEB approved; otherwise, your degree won’t be valid for government jobs or higher studies. 
  • Specialization: Make sure your electives (such as Data Analytics and FinTech) fit with your long-term job ambitions. 
  • LMS Quality: Make sure the Learning Management System works well on mobile devices and lets you watch recorded lectures at any time. 
  • Placement Support: Find colleges that have virtual job fairs, resume seminars, and partnerships with businesses. 
  • Faculty: Find out if the teachers are specialists in their field or have worked in the field for a long time.

Do you need GMAT for an online MBA?

Most online MBA programs in India don’t require the GMAT. Most of the time, admission is based on your grades from college, which should be at least 50%. 

  • Indian Private Universities: Top schools like Amity, Jain, and Manipal will let you in purely on your grades without making you take an entrance exam. 
  • Global/Elite Programs: You usually just need a GMAT score for top global programs like Harvard and INSEAD or certain Executive/Blended MBAs at IIMs.

How long does it take to get an MBA online?

A conventional online MBA in India takes two years to finish, with four semesters. This is the same length as a regular full-time program, which is required by UGC-DEB rules to make sure that all students are on the same level academically. 

But there are differences depending on how long you’ve been working: 

Executive Online MBA: This program is for mid- to senior-level professionals and usually takes 12 to 18 months to finish. 

Maximum Duration: Most colleges give students up to four years to accomplish their degree. This gives them a safety net in case employment or personal responsibilities get in the way.

How much does online MBA cost?

The total cost of an online MBA in India is usually between ₹60,000 and ₹4,00,000 for the whole two years. This is a lot less than the cost of a regular on-campus program. Open or publicly funded universities are usually the cheapest, costing less than ₹1 Lakh. Private universities normally charge between ₹1.5 Lakh and ₹3.5 Lakh. However, high-level online certifications from top national colleges might cost more than ₹10 Lakh because of their reputation and specialized curriculum.

How hard is an online MBA?

An online MBA is exactly as demanding academically as a normal one, but it feels “harder” for a different reason: discipline. 

The curriculum, tests, and projects are similar to those in traditional programs, but the hard part is balancing a 40-hour job week with 15 to 20 hours of study per week. You need to be able to start things on your own because there isn’t a physical classroom to keep you on track. You need to be able to manage your time well and stay motivated without the social pressure of being on campus in order to be successful.

Will an online MBA help my career?

You can get an online MBA that can help your career a lot by making you qualified for management jobs and pay raises without having to stop working. In today’s era, employers will value the digital abilities and practical talents that these programs teach. If the degree is authorized by the UGC-DEB, it is legally the same as a standard MBA for government positions, private promotions, and foreign prospects. It also has a good return on investment.

What are the eligibility criteria for online MBA?

Most of the time, you need to complete these three requirements to sign up for an online MBA in India: 

  • Academic Qualification: A bachelor’s degree from a recognized university in any field. 
  • Minimum Marks: Usually, you need to have at least 50% of your total marks in graduation (with a 5% relaxation for reserved categories). 
  • Entrance Exams: Most private colleges and universities admit you, depending on your grades. But certain top schools and certain executive programs may demand a genuine CAT, MAT, or GMAT score.

What is the average salary after completing an online MBA course?

Currently, the average pay for someone with an online MBA in India is between ₹5 Lakh and ₹15 Lakh per year. Starting packages for new employees normally range from ₹3 lakh to ₹6 lakh. But after finishing the degree, employed professionals generally get a rise of 30% to 60%. The final pay that recruiters offer is greatly affected by things including your selected field of study, your past work experience, and the university’s reputation.

Do I need to get a high CAT score to get admission into an online MBA program?

Most online MBA programs in India don’t require a high CAT score or any other entrance exam at all. Most private and public institutions let you in directly based on your undergraduate grades, which usually need to be at least 50%. Most of the time, you only need to take the CAT or GMAT if you want to get into a specialized online or executive program at a top national institute like the IIMs.

Do I need to take exams to clear my online MBA?
