Semester 1

Financial Accounting-I

Learners will gain a strong foundation in accounting principles and standards, along with hands-on experience in preparing financial statements for manufacturing and non-manufacturing sole proprietorships. The subject also covers key accounting treatments and introduces emerging trends shaping the accounting profession.

Business Organization and Management

Learners will gain an understanding of business concepts, organizational forms, and core management functions, along with the ability to apply them in real-world scenarios. The subject also builds critical thinking, ethical awareness, and decision-making skills for effective business practice.

Corporate Legal Environment

Learners will understand the legal framework governing business, including key laws related to contracts, companies, consumers, taxation, and partnerships. The subject also introduces consumer redressal mechanisms and the basics of international business laws.

Digital Fluency

Learners will understand core computer concepts and develop practical skills in using MS Office applications to support everyday business activities.

Communicative English

Learners will build strong communication, listening, speaking, and writing skills, including presentations, interviews, meetings, and group discussions. The subject also helps identify communication barriers and apply effective techniques in professional settings.
Semester 2

Financial Accounting - II

Learners will gain practical expertise in advanced accounting treatments, including consignment, joint ventures, hire purchase, branches, and inter-departmental transactions. The subject also covers partnership reconstitution, dissolution, insolvency, royalty accounting, and firm conversion.

Financial Literacy

Learners will understand financial literacy and key financial institutions, and learn to plan budgets and manage personal finances. The subject also covers banking, postal services, insurance planning, and basic investment decisions.

Economic Theory

Learners will understand core economic concepts, including demand and supply, elasticity, production, and revenue under different market structures. The subject also introduces national income, business cycles, inflation, and their impact on economic decision-making.

Environmental Studies

Learners will understand the scope of environmental studies, ecosystems, sustainability, and natural resource conservation. The subject also covers environmental pollution, climate change, and policies addressing major environmental challenges.

Functional English

Learners will develop a strong understanding of correct English usage while improving reading and writing skills across key language components.
Semester 3

Corporate Accounting

Learners will gain a comprehensive understanding of the Companies Act and corporate accounting practices, including share and debenture transactions. The subject also covers goodwill valuation and the preparation of final accounts.

Principles of Marketing

Learners will understand core marketing concepts, the marketing environment, and the difference between marketing and selling. The subject also covers marketing mix decisions, market segmentation, targeting, positioning, and promotion strategies.

Corporate Governance and Corporate Social Responsibilities

Learners will gain a thorough understanding of corporate governance, its evolution, mechanisms, and the Indian model. The subject also examines stakeholder responsibilities, investor protection, board roles, auditing, and social responsibility in business.

Basics of Statistics & Mathematics

Learners will understand the role of statistics and decision models in solving business problems while building knowledge of descriptive and inferential statistics. The subject also develops analytical skills for effective data analysis.

Personality Development and Leadership

Learners will develop self-awareness, confidence, and a growth mindset while building motivation and goal-setting skills. The subject also enhances communication, interpersonal abilities, leadership, time management, and non-verbal skills for personal and professional growth.
Semester 4

Cost Accounting

Learners will understand traditional and modern costing systems and their benefits in business decision-making. The subject also covers applying cost accounting methods across industries and reconciling cost and financial accounts.

Financial Markets, Institutions and Services

Learners will understand the structure and functions of central and commercial banking in India, along with key financial services and derivatives. The subject also covers stock exchange operations, regulations, and pension funds in India.

Entrepreneurship Development

Learners will understand entrepreneurship development by exploring support agencies, entrepreneurial skills, and idea generation methods. The subject also guides learners in preparing business plans and selecting suitable financing options for new ventures.

Management Accounting

Learners will understand the scope of management accounting and its link with financial and cost accounting. The subject also builds skills in financial analysis, cash and fund flow analysis, CVP analysis, budgeting, and decision-oriented costing techniques.

E-Business

Learners will understand the origin and evolution of e-commerce and how it differs from traditional business systems. The subject also covers e-commerce architecture, business and revenue models, and B2B electronic payment processes.
Semester 5

Direct Taxation and GST Law & Practice

Learners will develop application-oriented skills to understand and apply the provisions of the Income Tax Act, 1961 and GST. The subject also enables learners to compute tax liabilities, assess the impact of tax laws on business decisions, and ensure statutory compliance to manage risks effectively.

Human Resource Management

Learners will understand the fundamentals of Human Resource Management and key HR practices such as job analysis, recruitment, and selection. The subject also explores training and development methods to enhance employee performance and growth.

Principles and Practice of Auditing

Learners will gain an understanding of auditing principles, methods, and their practical applications. The subject also covers audit working papers, audit notebooks, verification of liabilities, and the basics of financial statements.

Fundamentals of Financial Management

Learners will understand the role of financial management in business decision-making and key financing options, including cost of capital and capital structure decisions. The subject also covers investment decisions and effective working capital management for financial stability.

International Financial Reporting Standards

Learners will understand accounting standards and the theoretical framework for preparing financial statements. The subject also builds skills to analyze and interpret financial statements effectively.
Semester 6

Investment Options & Mutual Funds

Learners will explore various investment alternatives and understand the fundamentals of investment analysis and valuation. The subject also highlights investor protection and informed investment decision-making.

Strategic Financial Management

Learners will understand how financial policies and decision-making impact value creation, capital budgeting, and risk management. The subject also covers securitization, business valuation, and strategies for value creation through mergers, acquisitions, and restructuring.

International Finance

Learners will understand the concepts, importance, and dynamics of international business, including India’s role in the global market. The subject also provides theoretical foundations relevant to global business operations and developments.

Business Research Methodology

Learners will understand the fundamentals of research, including types, processes, and problem formulation. The subject also covers hypothesis development, data collection methods, questionnaire design, attitude measurement, and sampling techniques.

Project Work

Learners will independently select a research guide from academia or industry and choose a project topic in a core area of commerce. A university-appointed research supervisor will monitor the progress of the project work.

Eligibility for online BCom

Indian students
NRI & Foreign students

Educational qualification

Candidates must have completed 10+2 from a recognized national or state board institution or 10+3 diploma from a recognized national or state institute.

Educational qualification

Candidates must have completed 10+2 / intermediate / senior secondary from a recognized board or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities in any discipline.

 

Candidates who have completed 10+2 education outside India must produce a certificate of equivalence issued by the Association of Indian Universities.

 

Other requirements

Candidates who are not Indian citizens or residing outside India and holding NRE or PIO status will be billed an international fee for respective degrees & may need to submit documents mentioned but not limited to CV, address proof, valid visa, PR card & passport copy as part of the application process.

Are international students eligible to apply for programs at Online Manipal?

Yes, subject to fulfilling eligibility criteria pertaining to the program you are applying for. Online Manipal accepts applications from both Indian and international students for all programs. Non-Indian applicants should apply via www.onlinemanipal.com/global

What is the application process for online degrees at Sikkim Manipal University? 

The application process is completely online and applications can be submitted at www.onlinemanipal.com by providing the required details. If you need further assistance, then you can reach out to our admission counsellors at +91-7996 799 900 and they will assist you with the application process.

*What are the terms & conditions for EMIs and other easy financing options? 

*Zero cost EMI option is available only on payment of annual fee.

Approval of loans from our loan partners depends on applicants meeting credit norms and submitting required documents. Loan partners may impose other fees and may have additional terms & conditions that must be met by applicants.

For further details on EMIs and other easy financing options, please contact our admission counsellors.

What is the application fee for Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs?

The application fee for Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs is INR 500 (inclusive of GST). This is a separate fee which is over and above the course fee. Please note that the application fee is non-refundable.

What is the selection process?

All applications will be evaluated for eligibility by the admissions team of Sikkim Manipal University. In case the university requires further clarification or documents, the candidate will be intimated via email, SMS or phone. Final decision regarding selection will be notified to the candidate within five working days of submitting the application.

Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

  • Roll number issued by the university
  • Aadhar number (12 digits)
  • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
  • Gender
  • Date of Birth
  • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@smu.onlinemanipal.com or call +91-7090 133 300 for any queries.

How will I know about the status of my admission? 

Once a decision has been taken on the application submitted, the status of admission would be intimated to the candidate via email and SMS.

More FAQs

Can students take online examinations from any device?

Students can take up online examinations from any desktop or laptop running a Windows Operating System with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed. The Safe Exam Browser (exam portal) is not compatible with iOS devices.

Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

What is the evaluation & assessment pattern? 

  • A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both continuous assessments (CA) through assignments and term-end examinations (TEE) which consist of descriptive and multiple-choice questions. A student must score at least 40% in each of these to be declared as having passed the exam. The assessment ratio of TEE to CA is 70:30.
  • A student must score at least 40% in both the parts to be declared as having passed.

What is the examination pattern for online degrees at Sikkim Manipal University? 

  • The University term-end examination (TEE) will be held digitally viz. computer-based and online proctored.
  • Candidates can appear for the exams from the comfort of their homes.
  • The details of the examination schedule will be made available in the photo admit card. Other details on the examination pattern are available in the program guide provided to students.
  • Examination results will be published on the student portal. If a student fails to clear any of the papers (either in UE or IA or both), he must re-appear by applying to the University through the re-sitting application along with relevant fees, within the due date.
  • On satisfying all criteria stipulated by the University, successful students will be awarded their respective program degree by the University.

What is the minimum grade to complete the online degree program? 

Students must score at least 40% in continuous evaluation and 40% in term-end examinations in each course to successfully complete the program. In case the minimum score is not attained, they will need to apply for re-sit with applicable fees and appear for exam to pass the course. 

More FAQs

How are Sikkim Manipal University’s online degree programs delivered? 

Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs are delivered in a 100% online mode so that students can attend live classes and complete the program remotely, while also pursuing their professional and personal commitments in parallel.

Will the program have live lectures and webinars? 

Yes. The program is delivered through a mix of live lectures, pre- recorded videos and discussion forums in which students can interact directly with faculty and self-paced learning. Self-paced learning is driven by video lectures, e-books, quizzes, and assignments that are delivered using a state-of-the-art Learning Management System (LMS).

Are there any mandatory sessions? 

Students are expected to attend live classes, watch recorded sessions, and go through at least 75% of the self-learning content (reading material and video lectures) available on LMS. This would ensure they are eligible to appear for the final exams.

When will I get access to the learning modules? 

After admission is confirmed by the university, students will receive access to the Learning Management System (LMS), student portal and e-library. Students can then start accessing the course material and begin their learning journey.

What are the study materials given to learners of online degree programs at Sikkim Manipal University? 

Students are given access to an exhaustive e-library, video lectures, audio-visual interactive material, virtual classroom sessions, audio podcasts, virtual simulations, and self-assessment quizzes or tests. Students are free to download e-books and print them for convenience.

How can I get my academic queries answered? 

Students will be assigned dedicated course mentors and coordinators who can be contacted to clarify doubts using discussion forums, instant messaging, and email on the Learning Management System (LMS).

How much time should I dedicate to complete the learning activities? 

Although the learning is flexible, students are expected to spend at least 15-20 hours per week watching concept videos, reading learning material, attempting quizzes, and attending live webinars. This would ensure that you get your degree within the minimum duration.
