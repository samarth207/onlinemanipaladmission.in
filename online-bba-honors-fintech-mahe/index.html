A FinTech (Financial Technology) specialization with this online Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA Honors) program elevates how technology is reinventing the financial services sector. It combines key tenets of businesses with technological advancements in finance such as cybersecurity in finance, financial modelling using R and Python, Blockchain Technology and Financial Web Technologies. This field of study will prepare learners in India with both fiscal knowledge and technical expertise to gear them up for roles in financial services, startups, and technology-based organizations. Students who meet graduation requirements at the end of the three-year BBA program and maintain a CGPA of 8.0 or above are eligible to pursue a fourth year and earn a BBA (Honors) degree, subject to an additional cost.     Learn More About Our BBA Program (FAQs)

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@onlinemanipaladmission.in for any queries.

    What is the duration of BBA (Honors) in Fintech program?

    Like an on-campus BBA program, the online BBA (Hons) also is a three-year degree program consisting of six semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 6 years.  

    Learners with CGPA of 8 or above at the end of the 6th semester of the online BBA program can opt for BBA (Honors) and take an additional one year to earn a BBA (Honors) degree. In that case, students can take up to 8 years to complete their degree.

    Are flexible payment options available for these courses? What are the eligibility criteria?  

    Yes, students have access to various payment methods. They can choose to pay the full program fee upfront, pay it annually, semester-wise, or use a no-cost EMI (for Indian students) facility with the bank that is financially partnered. 

    If I am eligible for a scholarship and also pay the entire fee upfront, can I avail both the scholarship and the fee concession (for full payment)?

    Yes. All learners who pay the full program fee upfront can avail 5% fee concession on total program fee upon approval. In addition to the fee concession, if a learner is eligible for a scholarship, he/she can avail the same. The scholarship amount will be calculated on the total program fee.

    If I am funding my fee through a bank loan, can I apply for a fee concession for upfront payment of full program fee?

    • Fee concessions for upfront payment apply only when the program fee is paid by the learner through their own source of funds, such as their own bank account or card payment.
    • Learners who take an independent bank education loan (without subvention or zero-interest EMI) are eligible for this concession.
    • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.
