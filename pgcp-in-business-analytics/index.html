    Semester 1

    Financial Reporting & Statement Analysis

    This subject provides a strong foundation in financial reporting and statement analysis, covering regulatory frameworks, the accounting cycle, accrual adjustments, cash flow preparation, asset and liability reporting, and key techniques for evaluating profitability, risk, and accounting quality.

    Business Statistics

    This subject introduces core business statistics concepts, including descriptive analysis, probability and uncertainty, key probability distributions, sampling methods, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, and regression techniques for data-driven decision-making.

    Managerial Economics

    This subject covers demand analysis and forecasting, supply and cost concepts, pricing decisions in competitive and imperfect markets, and core macroeconomic topics such as consumption, investment, business cycles, and inflation.

    Programming with R & Python

    This subject helps to build foundational skills in R and Python for data analysis, visualization, and data manipulation using libraries like tidyverse, NumPy, and Pandas.

    Database Management

    This subject introduces core concepts of databases, covering data models, ER design, SQL querying, normalization, transaction processing, concurrency control, query optimization, and modern topics like data warehousing and big data systems.
    Semester 2

    Business Econometrics

    This subject covers regression-based techniques to analyze economic and business data, including estimation, hypothesis testing, model issues, and advanced applications like logistic and simultaneous equation models.

    Financial Management

    This subject introduces core financial decision-making concepts, covering planning, valuation, cost of capital, capital budgeting, working capital management, and dividend policies to support effective corporate finance strategies.

    Applied Multivariate Data Analysis

    This subject equips learners with an understanding of multivariate distributions statistics, factor PCA analysis, cluster discriminant methods, multidimensional scaling, and conjoint canonical correlation.

    Data Visualisation

    This subject introduces foundational visualization principles, data transformation and interactive dashboarding within Power BI, Tableau’s advanced analytics, and features to craft compelling data-driven stories.

    Design and Analysis of Experiments

    This subject provides core understanding of experimental principles, prognostic control through randomized and blocked designs, Latin squares and BIBDs, factorial interactions, advanced split-plot, and repeated measures methodologies.

    Mini Project

    This project encourages learners to explore practical applications of individual subjects, helping them connect academic concepts with professional contexts.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email helpdesk@manipalacademyonline.edu.in for any queries.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    Students can take up online examinations from any desktop/laptop with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed.

    Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

    No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    The assessment system has been conceived, developed and administered on a rigorous and fair basis to bring out the best in students and prepare them for a successful career.

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both Internal Assessment (IA) and End Term Examination (ETE). The assessment ratio of IA to ETE is 30:70.

    Internal assessment is based on learner’s participation in discussion board, performance in assignments, performance in unit wise graded quizzes and percentage of content access.

    At the end of each semester, the student is required to appear for the End Term Proctored Online Examination.

    A student must score at least 40% in Internal Assessment (IA) to become eligible for End Term Examination (ETE).

    A student must score at least 40% term-end examination to be declared as having passed in a subject.

    What is the grading pattern?

    The university uses 10 Point Grading System – A+ (10), A (9), B (8), C (7), D (6), E (5), F-Fail (0)

    What if I fail to clear some papers/subjects?

    If you fail to clear any paper/subject in End Term Exam (ETE), you must apply for re-sit exams whenever the University conducts by paying a requisite fees/paper.

    If I am funding my fee through a bank loan, can I apply for a fee concession for upfront payment of full program fee?

    • Fee concessions for upfront payment apply only when the program fee is paid by the learner through their own source of funds, such as their own bank account or card payment.
    • Learners who take an independent bank education loan (without subvention or zero-interest EMI) are eligible for this concession.
    • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.
