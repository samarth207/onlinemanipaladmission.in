    Semester 1

    Fundamentals of Computers and IT

    Learners will study the fundamentals of computers, including evolution, generations, architecture, CPU, memory, storage, input/output devices, software, operating systems, and communication systems. The course also covers number systems, Boolean logic, logical circuits, and Internet and networking concepts.

    Fundamentals of Mathematics

    This subject introduces learners to the essential mathematical concepts used in computing. It covers calculus, vectors, matrices, complex numbers, discrete mathematics, logic, graph theory, probability, and statistics. Emphasis is placed on both continuous and discrete mathematics, providing a strong foundation for problem-solving, programming, and advanced computer science topics.

    Discrete Mathematics and Graph Theory

    This course introduces learners to Python programming, covering core concepts such as syntax, variables, control structures, functions, and data structures. It also explores file handling, exception management, object-oriented programming, and applications in web development, data science, and game development, while emphasizing best practices for coding, testing, and deployment.

    Python Programming

    This course introduces learners to Python programming, covering core concepts such as syntax, variables, control structures, functions, and data structures. It also explores file handling, exception management, object-oriented programming, and applications in web development, data science, and game development, while emphasizing best practices for coding, testing, and deployment.

    Programming and Problem Solving in C

    This course provides a foundation in C programming, covering basic syntax, data types, operators, control structures, functions, arrays, strings, pointers, and structures. Learners will also explore file handling, dynamic memory allocation, advanced pointers, and graphical programming, developing practical skills to design, implement, and debug C programs.

    Relational Database Management System

    This course covers the concepts, architectures, and applications of modern database systems. It includes a comparison of database types (centralized, distributed, graph, time-series, RDBMS, NoSQL), relational databases and SQL fundamentals, query optimization and execution techniques, adaptive query processing, and transaction management with concurrency control. Students also learn about parallel and object-oriented databases, distributed databases, and so on.

    Data Visualisation

    Students learn about the principles, techniques, and tools for effective data visualization. They also learn visualization using Excel (basic and advanced chart types, statistical and specialized charts) and Python (Matplotlib, Seaborn, Plotly, Dash), including text, correlation, geographical, and time series data. The course also covers data cleaning and preparation, advanced visual techniques like 3D plots, and so on.

    Semester 2

    Core Subjects

    Computer Networks and Protocols

    This course introduces the fundamental concepts of computer networks, covering network models, protocols, routing, switching, security, and performance management. Students learn how data is transmitted, managed, and secured across modern networks, enabling learners to understand, analyze, and work with real-world networking systems and technologies.

    Object Oriented Programming using Java

    This subject equips learners with practical knowledge of Java, including object-oriented programming, multithreading, database connectivity, and web development. Students gain the ability to build end-to-end applications using modern Java ecosystems.

    Operating System

    Designed to provide a deep understanding of operating system principles, this subject explores process management, memory management, scheduling, file systems, storage, security, and distributed systems. Learners gain the ability to analyze and understand how modern operating systems manage hardware resources and support efficient, secure, and concurrent computing.

    Data Structure and Algorithms

    Focused on efficient problem-solving, this subject covers core data structures and algorithms, including lists, stacks, trees, graphs, searching, and sorting techniques. Learners develop the ability to analyze algorithm complexity and apply advanced data structures to design optimized and scalable solutions.

    Computer Architecture

    Centered on modern computing systems, this subject explores computer architecture principles, instruction set design, pipelining, parallel processing, memory hierarchy, and scalable architectures. Learners gain insight into how high-performance processors are designed and optimized for parallelism, efficiency, and real-world computing demands.

Learners develop the ability to plan, build, evaluate, and maintain high-quality software systems using systematic and industry-aligned methodologies.

    Eligibility for online MCA

    Indian students
    NRI & Foreign students

    Educational qualification

    Candidates must have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree in Computer Applications/Computer Science/Information Technology from a recognized university/institution, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU) or other competent bodies

     

    Candidates who have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree from other streams like Science, Business Administration, Business Management, Arts & Humanities, Commerce, etc. are also eligible for MCA admission but they have to attend and complete a compulsory bridge course in fundamentals of computer and IT along with their Semester 1 courses. Additionally, candidates who have not studied mathematics during their 10 + 2/ bachelor’s degree programs must attend and complete a compulsory bridge course in mathematics along with other semester 1 courses.

    Grades

    Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate in graduation (45% for reserved categories).

    Learn More About Our MCA Program (FAQs)

    What is the duration of this MCA program?

    Like an on-campus MCA, the online MCA is a two-year degree program consisting of four semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 4 years.

    What is the admission process for this online MCA degree?

    Here’s a guide to help you begin your online MCA 

    • Step 1: Fill in your basic, education & work experience-related details and pay the application fee to register.  
    • Step 2: Pay the admission fee for the first semester/year or full program.  
    • Step 3: Upload supporting documents and submit your application.   
    • Step 4: The university will evaluate your documents to confirm your admission. 

    Who should opt for online MCA courses?

    This program is ideal for candidates who have a degree in computer science, IT, math, or a related field and want to learn new skills or move up in their current IT job. It’s great for people who want to move up in their tech careers. 

    What are the documents required for admission into the Master of Computer Application course?

    Mandatory documents: 

    • Proof of identity – Aadhaar card (mandatory for Indian citizens) 
    • Proof of address – Aadhaar card, passport, voter’s ID, gas bill, bank statement, post-paid mobile statement 
    • Class 10 & 12 marksheet 
    • Graduation marksheet (for PG students) 

    Non-mandatory documents: 

    • CAT / GMAT / MAT valid score for MBA 
    • Work experience certificate 
    • Graduation degree certificate for PG students 
    • Scholarship documents 
    • Defense Service certificate 
    • Divyaang certificate 
    • Reserved Category certificate 
    • Government ID card 

    Is work experience mandatory for this MCA program? What qualifies work experience?

    The online MCA program does not require work experience. Though it is always good to have work experience as it can provide context to the learning and also make classroom discussions more interesting.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credit. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national need to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email to info@muj.onlinemanipal.com for any queries.

    What is the eligibility criteria for this Online MCA course? Who can apply?

    Educational qualification 

    Candidates must have a 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree in Computer Applications / Computer Science / Information Technology from a recognized university/institution, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities (AIU) or other competent bodies.

    Candidates who have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree in other streams like Science, Business Administration, Business Management, Arts & Humanities, Commerce, etc. are also eligible for MCA admission but they have to attend and complete a compulsory bridge course in fundamentals of computer and IT along with their Semester 1 courses. Additionally, candidates who have not studied mathematics during their 10 + 2 / bachelor’s degree programs must attend and complete a compulsory bridge course in mathematics along with other semester 1 courses.

    Grades

    Candidates must have a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate in graduation (45% for reserved categories).

    Are flexible payment options available for these courses? What is the eligibility criteria?

    Definitely, students can use education loans or payment plans in installments, which will help them to manage their financial commitment better over the period of the course.

    If I am eligible for a scholarship and pay the entire fee upfront, can I avail both the scholarship and the fee concession (for full payment)?

    Yes. All learners who pay the full program fee upfront can avail 10% fee concession on total program fee upon approval. In addition to the fee concession, if a learner is eligible for a scholarship, he/she can avail the same. The scholarship amount will be calculated on the total program fee.

    How will scholarships be processed for eligible candidates?

    Once a candidate’s scholarship is approved during admission, the scholarship benefit shall be applied as per the below table-

    Periodicity of fee payment Semester 1 Semester 2 Semester 3 Semester 4
    Semester-wise payment     – 1st and 2nd semester scholarship shall be applicable 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Annual payment – – 1st , 2nd and 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Full fee payment at the beginning of the program Scholarship applicable for all semesters of the program shall get refunded.

    For example- In the case of semester-wise fee payment, Semester-1 scholarship benefit shall be applicable in the subsequent semester ie- scholarship benefit of both Semester 1 & 2 shall be applicable while registering for Semester – 2. Thereafter, from Semester – 3, the current semester scholarship benefit shall be applicable.

    If I am funding my fee through a bank loan, can I apply for a fee concession for upfront payment of full program fee?

    • Fee concessions for upfront payment apply only when the program fee is paid by the learner through their own source of funds, such as their own bank account or card payment.
    • Learners who take an independent bank education loan (without subvention or zero-interest EMI) are eligible for this concession.
    • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.
