    Online MA Course curriculum

    Explore list of all subjects (semester wise) covered in our Master of Arts (MA) in Economics program

    Semester 1

    Microeconomics -I

    Learners will explore how microeconomic principles shape market behavior and learn to recognize these theoretical frameworks operating in everyday economic situations. The subject also aims to build foundational knowledge while connecting abstract economic theories to real-world contexts.

    Macroeconomics- I

    Learners will develop an understanding of how monetary policy functions, examining the goals, instruments, and channels through which central banks manage money supply, interest rates, and lending conditions to meet broader economic objectives. Learners also learn to evaluate fiscal policy frameworks, exploring how government expenditure, tax systems, and budget imbalances influence overall demand, economic expansion, and price stability, while recognizing the constraints inherent in these approaches.

    Fundamentals of Mathematics and Statistics

    Learners will gain proficiency in the principles and techniques of differential and integral calculus. The course explores practical applications of matrices and linear programming problems. Learners will be familiarized with the significance of statistical methods, including correlation and regression analysis, and hypothesis testing procedures such as F-tests and chi-square tests.

    Indian Economy

    The course explores the foundational concepts related to India's economic structure, and developmental patterns and trends within the agricultural sector. Learners get to analyze the evolution and growth trajectories of India's industrial sector. Additionally, they develop the ability to evaluate the function and influence of the World Trade Organization in global trade dynamics.

    Public Economics and Policy

    Learners will comprehend the fundamental principles of public economics. The course enables them examine and compare approaches taken by public and private sectors focusing on taxation systems and associated policy frameworks. Additionally, learners will understand the various revenue sources and spending patterns within the public sector.
    Semester 2

    Microeconomics -II

    Learners will gain foundational knowledge of key microeconomic theories, concepts, and analytical methods. The course will develop learners' understanding of how microeconomic principles influence market operations and enable them to recognize the application of these concepts in everyday economic scenarios.

    Macroeconomics -II

    Learners will develop understanding of macroeconomic concepts, frameworks, and theoretical approaches to comprehend how macroeconomic principles impact the broader economy. The course will examine connections between various economic models and sustained economic growth and development, emphasizing the significance of macroeconomic policy measures and their interconnected nature.

    Intermediate Econometrics

    Learners comprehend the fundamental principles of econometrics. The course equips them with the ability to utilize various econometric methods and techniques to accomplish organizational goals.

    History of Economic Thought

    This subject enhances learners' comprehension of the historical foundations and scope of economics as a discipline. It provides them with an opportunity to explore the development of economic thought, examining how theoretical frameworks have progressed and been refined over time.

    Economics of Innovation and Entrepreneurship

    Learners will explore the core economic considerations facing entrepreneurs seeking to bring innovations to market through new ventures or existing organizations. The subject integrates essential principles from economics, strategic management, and related fields concerning technological progress and innovation, while maintaining a practical, management-oriented approach.
    Semester 3

    Application of Data Science in Economics

    This subject aims to develop learners' analytical comprehension of data science applications within economics. It provides them with opportunities to explore data science concepts, various analytical methodologies, and their economic applications, while examining the evolution and refinement of related theoretical frameworks over time.

    Environmental Economics

    Learners will comprehend the fundamental principles and scope of environmental economics. The course will enable learners to critically analyze how economic choices, market dynamics, and governmental policy frameworks influence environmental outcomes.

    Development Economics

    Learners will gain knowledge of various developmental approaches and theoretical frameworks. The course will familiarize learners with key challenges and concerns related to development. Additionally, learners will explore contemporary scholarly contributions and emerging perspectives in the field of development studies.

    Game Theory

    Learners will be introduced to game theory and its extensive applications within economics. The subject will present essential game-theoretic tools and equilibrium concepts through various exercises and practical applications. Acquiring game theory knowledge will enable learners to comprehend and evaluate real-world scenarios, including market dynamics and strategic behavior.

    Term Paper

    Learners will conduct a literature review providing a comprehensive summary of research on a selected topic. The review should draw from articles, books, eBooks, and relevant journals, offering objective description, summary, and evaluation of the content while establishing a theoretical foundation for future research.

    Elective I (Choose any one)

    Health Economics

    Learners will be introduced to the fundamental concepts of health economics. The subject will familiarize them with various theoretical frameworks and models within health economics. Additionally, learners will develop competencies to make informed decisions in health economics and related functional domains.

    Behavioural Economics

    Learners will gain comprehensive understanding of behavioural economics and its integration of psychological insights into economic analysis. They develop analytical skills to evaluate behavioral economic theories and their applications in various contexts. Additionally, learners will assess the relevance of behavioral approaches in addressing contemporary economic issues and policy challenges.

    Agriculture Economics

    Learners will assess patterns and developments in agricultural output and efficiency. The course will examine major theoretical perspectives on agricultural transformation. Learners will also comprehend the financial dimensions of agriculture, encompassing credit distribution and funding structures. Additionally, the course will enable understanding of national agricultural policies and their broader impacts.
    Semester 4

    International Economics

    Learners will explore international trade, origins of trade benefits, and how these gains are distributed domestically and globally, while examining trade policy tools such as tariffs and quantitative restrictions and their effects. The course will equip them with analytical techniques to determine appropriate models and approaches for interpreting world economic events, fostering critical analysis of global developments that influence the international economic landscape.

    Monetary Economics

    The subject will enable learners understand India's financial system and its constituent elements. They will examine the dynamics of money demand and supply, and analyze theoretical frameworks and contemporary reforms pertaining to financial developments.

    Operation Research in Economics

    Learners will understand how operations research connects with other essential functional domains within organizations. The course will develop comprehensive knowledge of operations research methodologies and their practical applications across various fields to facilitate effective decision-making processes.

    Dissertation

    Learners conduct dissertation research, and select a six-month project in their area of specialization, which may be completed at their workplace or an alternate location. This project component carries a weightage of six credits toward program completion.

    Elective II (Choose any one)

    Labor Economics

    Learners will comprehend the characteristics and functioning of labour markets through the study of labour economics. The course will examine topics including employment dynamics, wage-setting mechanisms, industrial relations, and the function of trade unions. Additionally, learners will analyze the state government's role in enhancing labour standards and working conditions.

    Economics of Human Development

    This subject explores the economic aspects of human development and well-being. Learners will examine the relationship between economic factors and improvements in health, education, and living standards. The course highlights how economic policies and strategies can enhance human capabilities and promote equitable development outcomes.

    International Financial System

    Learners acquire understanding of exchange rates, payments structure, foreign exchange market operations, trading instruments (spot, forward, and options contracts), and how market forces influence exchange rate movements.

    Learn More About Our MA Program (FAQs)

    What is the eligibility criteria for an Online MA in Economics course? Who can apply?

    Educational qualification  

    Candidates must have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree from a recognized university/institution or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities. 

    Candidates who have completed 10+2 education or graduate level education outside India must produce a certificate of equivalence issued by the Association of Indian Universities. 

    Other requirements 

    Candidates who are not Indian citizens or residing outside India and holding NRE or PIO status will be billed an international fee for respective degrees & may need to submit documents mentioned but not limited to CV, address proof, valid visa, PR card & passport copy as part of the application process.

    What is the duration of the MA in Economics program?

    Like an on-campus MA Economics program, the online MA also is a two-year degree program consisting of four semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 4 years.

    Who should opt for an online MA in Economics course?

    Anyone who is looking to enhance their career, switch fields, or study while working can definitely consider it. It is a great option for people who are not able to go to physical classes because of lack of time or other commitments.

    Is work experience mandatory for MA program? What qualifies as work experience?

    The online MA in Economics program at Manipal University Jaipur does not require work experience. Though it is always good to have work experience as it can provide context to the learning and also make classroom discussions more interesting.

    Are flexible payment options available for these courses? What is the eligibility criteria?

    Yes, students have access to various payment methods. Apart from upfront fee payments, learners can also choose to pay it annually, semester-wise, or use a no-cost EMI (for Indian students) facility with the bank that is financially partnered.

    Are online MA classes more affordable than regular MA classes?

    Yes, they are more affordable because of lower tuition and no relocation or living expenses. With flexible payment options, they are more affordable.

    If I am eligible for a scholarship and pay the entire fee upfront, can I avail both the scholarship and the fee concession (for full payment)?

    Yes. All learners who pay the full program fee upfront can avail 10% fee concession on total program fee upon approval. In addition to the fee concession, if a learner is eligible for a scholarship, he/she can avail the same. The scholarship amount will be calculated on the total program fee.

    How will scholarships be processed for eligible candidates?

    Once a candidate’s scholarship is approved during admission, the scholarship benefit shall be applied as per the below table-

    Periodicity of fee payment Semester 1 Semester 2 Semester 3 Semester 4
    Semester-wise payment     – 1st and 2nd semester scholarship shall be applicable 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Annual payment – – 1st , 2nd and 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Full fee payment at the beginning of the program Scholarship applicable for all semesters of the program shall get refunded.

    For example- In the case of semester-wise fee payment, Semester-1 scholarship benefit shall be applicable in the subsequent semester ie- scholarship benefit of both Semester 1 & 2 shall be applicable while registering for Semester – 2. Thereafter, from Semester – 3, the current semester scholarship benefit shall be applicable.

    If I am funding my fee through a bank loan, can I apply for a fee concession for upfront payment of full program fee?

    • Fee concessions for upfront payment apply only when the program fee is paid by the learner through their own source of funds, such as their own bank account or card payment.
    • Learners who take an independent bank education loan (without subvention or zero-interest EMI) are eligible for this concession.
    • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.

    *What are the terms & conditions for EMIs and other easy financing options?

    *EMI amount mentioned above is applicable for the entire program fee and would need to be paid monthly, for the full duration of the program.

    Approval of loans from our loan partners depends on applicants meeting credit norms and submitting required documents. Loan partners may impose other fees and may have additional terms & conditions that must be met by applicants.

    For further details on EMIs and other easy financing options, please contact our admission counsellors.

    More FAQs

    Will there be exams for an online MA in Economics program?

    The students are required to take term-end exams, and any other applicable assessments to fulfil the criteria to be eligible for award of degree.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    Students can take up online examinations from any desktop or laptop running a Windows Operating System with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed. The Safe Exam Browser (exam portal) is not compatible with iOS devices.

    Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

    No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

    What is the examination pattern for online degrees at Manipal University Jaipur?

    The University term-end examination (TEE) will be held digitally viz. computer-based and online proctored.

    Candidates can appear for the exams from the comfort of their homes.

    The details of the examination schedule will be made available in the photo admit card. Other details on the examination pattern are available in the program guide provided to students.

    Examination results will be published on the student portal. If a student fails to clear any of the papers (either in TEE or IA or both), he must re-appear by applying to the University through the re-sitting application along with relevant fees, within the due date.

    On satisfying all criteria stipulated by the University, successful students will be awarded their respective program degree by the University.

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both continuous assessments (CA) through assignments and term-end examinations (TEE) which consist of descriptive and multiple-choice questions. A student must score at least 40% in each of these to be declared as having passed the exam. The assessment ratios of TEE to CA is 70:30.

    Practical papers (as applicable)

    The assessment in the practical part is based on performance in guided exercises (i.e., day-to-day exercises) and unguided exercises. The latter will be conducted on the last day of the practical sessions in each subject with an external examiner.

    A student must score at least 40% in both the parts to be declared as having passed.

    What is the minimum grade to complete the online degree program?

    Students must score at least 40% in continuous evaluation and 40% in term-end examinations in each course to successfully complete the program. In case the minimum score is not attained, they will need to apply for a re-sit with applicable fees and appear for particular exam to pass the course.

    Can online degrees from Manipal University Jaipur be recognized in all regions of Canada?

    Yes, Manipal University Jaipur’s degrees are recognized in Canada. Here are the bodies that evaluates Manipal University Jaipur’s degrees for issuing equivalence certificates . These bodies conduct educational credential assessment (ECA) services for both employers and higher education institutions (HEIs).

    World Education Services (WES) – Applicable across US and Canada.
    International Credential Assessment Service of Canada (ICAS)- Applicable across Canada for employment and immigration purposes. ICAS is the only accepted evaluation service for admission to colleges in Ontario province.
    International Credential Evaluation Service (ICES)- The government-mandated ECA service in British Columbia which also serves the interest of all internationally educated applicants throughout Canada.
