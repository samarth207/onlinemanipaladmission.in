Semester 1

Management Concepts and Organizational Behavior

This subject introduces the principles and scope of management, exploring its functions, roles, and skills required for effective leadership. It also examines the evolution of management thought and applies key concepts such as planning, organizing, staffing, leading, and controlling in organizational contexts.

Marketing Management

This subject introduces the fundamentals of marketing, covering its concepts, evolution, and significance in business. It also examines consumer behaviour, the marketing mix, and product management strategies to enable effective marketing decision-making.

Financial Accounting & Reporting

This subject introduces the fundamentals of marketing, covering its concepts, evolution, and significance in business. It also examines consumer behaviour, the marketing mix, and product management strategies to enable effective marketing decision-making.

Economics for Managers

This subject introduces the fundamentals of economics, helping students analyse business situations and apply economic tools for decision-making. It covers consumer behaviour, production and cost analysis, market structures, and key macroeconomic concepts relevant to economic choices.

Corporate Financial Management

This subject introduces the principles of corporate financial management, covering financial planning, capital structure, and investment decisions. It also examines capital budgeting, working capital management, and valuation techniques to support effective financial decision-making in organizations.
Semester 2

Advanced Corporate Accounting

This subject familiarizes students with the preparation and analysis of company financial statements under Ind AS. It also covers processes related to share issuance and redemption, capital restructuring, and company amalgamations in accordance with the Companies Act, 2013.

Business and Economic Laws

This subject introduces the fundamental principles of business law, focusing on legal aspects of starting and operating a business. It equips students to interpret, apply, and communicate legal requirements, resolve real-world business legal issues, and ensure compliance throughout the business lifecycle.

Cost Analysis & Control

This subject introduces the principles and practices of cost accounting, focusing on cost analysis, pricing decisions, and overhead management. It also covers techniques like CVP analysis and activity-based management to support informed business decision-making and efficient resource use.

Human Resource Management

This subject introduces the fundamentals of Human Resource Management, covering recruitment, selection, talent management, and skill development in organizations. It also focuses on performance management, training, career development, and designing HRM processes including compensation and reward systems.

Audit & Assurance

This subject introduces the principles and concepts of auditing, including its nature, types, and objectives. It also covers generally accepted auditing procedures and techniques, equipping students with the skills to conduct audit and attestation engagements effectively.
Semester 3

Business Ethics and Corporate Governance

This subject introduces the fundamentals of business ethics, including principles, code of conduct, and corporate governance practices. It also explores Indian ethos, self-management, investor protection, and relevant laws to promote ethical and responsible business practices.

Strategic Management

This subject introduces the concept of business strategy and the role of strategic management in organizational decision-making. It also covers strategic analysis, environmental scanning, strategy implementation, and the development of strategic leadership for achieving competitive advantage.

Management Accounting

This subject explores advanced cost and management accounting techniques, including marginal, absorption, and activity-based costing, and their impact on financial reporting and decision-making. It also covers break-even analysis, product mix decisions, and the use of relevant financial and qualitative information for effective business decisions.

Elective: Finance

Management of Financial Services

This subject introduces the nature and role of financial services in economic development, focusing on institutions like merchant banks and credit rating agencies. It also examines various financial instruments, their features, and equips students with skills to manage financial service operations effectively.

Security Analysis and Portfolio Management

This subject provides a foundational understanding of investments, financial markets, instruments, and participants. It also covers valuation and analysis techniques, portfolio management concepts, and the practical application of tools for selecting, evaluating, and managing investment portfolios.

Elective: Marketing

E-Business

This subject introduces the fundamentals of e-business, exploring its origin, history, and differences from traditional business systems. It also covers e-business frameworks, business and revenue models, and the functioning of electronic B2B payment systems.

Consumer Behavior

This subject examines consumer behaviour, exploring how personal, socio-cultural, and environmental factors influence buying decisions. It also equips students to design and evaluate marketing strategies based on insights into consumer behavior.
Semester 4

Research Methodology and Statistical Analysis

This subject introduces the fundamentals of research, covering its meaning, objectives, and significance across fields. It also focuses on research design, measurement, validity, reliability, and sampling techniques to ensure accurate data collection and analysis.

Project Work

This subject involves independent project work in commerce, where students select a research guide, choose a topic from a core area, and carry out a research project. Progress is monitored by an appointed university supervisor to ensure academic rigor and practical relevance.

Elective: Finance

Corporate Tax Laws & Planning

This subject introduces the principles and historical development of income tax law in India, covering key concepts such as capital and revenue income. It also focuses on residential status, tax exemptions, allowances, perquisites, and the computation of income from house property.

Merger & Acquisitions

This subject introduces corporate restructuring through mergers and acquisitions, covering procedures, valuation methods, and accounting treatments. It also examines the legal and regulatory framework, takeover defenses, and post-merger integration processes.

Elective: Marketing

International Marketing

This subject introduces the fundamentals of international marketing, focusing on major issues, global market trends, and modern marketing practices. It also equips students to analyse an organization’s potential to enter and compete effectively in international markets.

Advertising Management and Sales Promotion

This subject focuses on advertising and marketing communications, teaching students to align campaigns with client objectives. It also covers market segmentation, target audience analysis, consumer behaviour, and evaluating the effectiveness of marketing initiatives.

Are international students eligible to apply for programs at Online Manipal?

Yes, subject to fulfilling eligibility criteria pertaining to the program you are applying for. Online Manipal accepts applications from both Indian and international students for all programs. Non-Indian applicants should apply via www.onlinemanipaladmission.in/global

What is the application process for online degrees at Sikkim Manipal University? 

The application process is completely online and applications can be submitted at www.onlinemanipaladmission.in by providing the required details. If you need further assistance, then you can reach out to our admission counsellors at +91-8920 785 477 and they will assist you with the application process.

*What are the terms & conditions for EMIs and other easy financing options? 

*Zero cost EMI option is available only on payment of annual fee.

The EMI amount mentioned above applies to the annual fee and must be paid monthly for 12 months.

Approval of loans from our loan partners depends on applicants meeting credit norms and submitting required documents. Loan partners may impose other fees and may have additional terms & conditions that must be met by applicants.

For further details on EMIs and other easy financing options, please contact our admission counsellors.

What is the application fee for Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs?

The application fee for Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs is INR 500 (inclusive of GST). This is a separate fee which is over and above the course fee. Please note that the application fee is non-refundable.

What is the selection process?

All applications will be evaluated for eligibility by the admissions team of Sikkim Manipal University. In case the university requires further clarification or documents, the candidate will be intimated via email, SMS or phone. Final decision regarding selection will be notified to the candidate within five working days of submitting the application.

Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

  • Roll number issued by the university
  • Aadhar number (12 digits)
  • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
  • Gender
  • Date of Birth
  • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@onlinemanipaladmission.in or call +91-8920 785 477 for any queries.

How will I know about the status of my admission? 

Once a decision has been taken on the application submitted, the status of admission would be intimated to the candidate via email and SMS.

More FAQs

Can students take online examinations from any device?

Students can take up online examinations from any desktop or laptop running a Windows Operating System with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed. The Safe Exam Browser (exam portal) is not compatible with iOS devices.

Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

What is the evaluation & assessment pattern? 

  • A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both continuous assessments (CA) through assignments and term-end examinations (TEE) which consist of descriptive and multiple-choice questions. A student must score at least 40% in each of these to be declared as having passed the exam. The assessment ratio of TEE to CA is 70:30.
  • A student must score at least 40% in both the parts to be declared as having passed.

What is the examination pattern for online degrees at Sikkim Manipal University? 

  • The University term-end examination (TEE) will be held digitally viz. computer-based and online proctored.
  • Candidates can appear for the exams from the comfort of their homes.
  • The details of the examination schedule will be made available in the photo admit card. Other details on the examination pattern are available in the program guide provided to students.
  • Examination results will be published on the student portal. If a student fails to clear any of the papers (either in UE or IA or both), he must re-appear by applying to the University through the re-sitting application along with relevant fees, within the due date.
  • On satisfying all criteria stipulated by the University, successful students will be awarded their respective program degree by the University.

What is the minimum grade to complete the online degree program? 

Students must score at least 40% in continuous evaluation and 40% in term-end examinations in each course to successfully complete the program. In case the minimum score is not attained, they will need to apply for re-sit with applicable fees and appear for exam to pass the course. 

More FAQs

How are Sikkim Manipal University’s online degree programs delivered? 

Sikkim Manipal University’s online programs are delivered in a 100% online mode so that students can attend live classes and complete the program remotely, while also pursuing their professional and personal commitments in parallel.

Will the program have live lectures and webinars? 

Yes. The program is delivered through a mix of live lectures, pre- recorded videos and discussion forums in which students can interact directly with faculty and self-paced learning. Self-paced learning is driven by video lectures, e-books, quizzes, and assignments that are delivered using a state-of-the-art Learning Management System (LMS).

Are there any mandatory sessions? 

Students are expected to attend live classes, watch recorded sessions, and go through at least 75% of the self-learning content (reading material and video lectures) available on LMS. This would ensure they are eligible to appear for the final exams.

When will I get access to the learning modules? 

After admission is confirmed by the university, students will receive access to the Learning Management System (LMS), student portal and e-library. Students can then start accessing the course material and begin their learning journey.

What are the study materials given to learners of online degree programs at Sikkim Manipal University? 

Students are given access to an exhaustive e-library, video lectures, audio-visual interactive material, virtual classroom sessions, audio podcasts, virtual simulations, and self-assessment quizzes or tests. Students are free to download e-books and print them for convenience.

How can I get my academic queries answered? 

Students will be assigned dedicated course mentors and coordinators who can be contacted to clarify doubts using discussion forums, instant messaging, and email on the Learning Management System (LMS).

How much time should I dedicate to complete the learning activities? 

Although the learning is flexible, students are expected to spend at least 15-20 hours per week watching concept videos, reading learning material, attempting quizzes, and attending live webinars. This would ensure that you get your degree within the minimum duration.
