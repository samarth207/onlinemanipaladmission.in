    Semester 1

    Fundamentals of Mathematics

    This subject covers the basics of functions, limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration. It focuses on understanding how functions behave and how calculus is used to solve problems related to change, optimization, and area under curves. The course builds a strong foundation for further studies in mathematics and its practical applications.

    Technical Communication

    This subject develops effective communication skills by covering the basics of verbal and non-verbal communication, vocabulary, and grammar. It focuses on professional writing, job-related communication, and presentation skills to help students communicate confidently in academic and workplace settings.

    C Programming

    This subject introduces the fundamentals of C programming, including program structure, data types, operators, and control statements. It covers arrays, strings, and functions to help students develop logical thinking and problem-solving skills. The course builds a strong foundation for writing structured and efficient programs using C.

    Fundamentals of Computers & Digital Systems

    The course explains how computers work internally, covering hardware components, memory, storage, and input–output devices. It introduces number systems, Boolean algebra, logic gates, and digital circuits. Students gain a clear understanding of combinational and sequential circuits used in modern computing systems

    Introduction to Web Programming

    This course introduces the fundamentals of web development using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It covers web page design, forms, data handling with JSON, APIs, and basic concepts of HTTP and e-commerce. Students also learn essential principles of web accessibility and modern web practices for creating user-friendly websites.

    Environmental Science

    Learners explore environmental science as a multidisciplinary field covering ecosystems, natural resources, biodiversity, and sustainability. The syllabus examines pollution, climate change, conservation strategies, and environmental laws. It also highlights the relationship between human communities and the environment, emphasizing responsible resource use and environmental awareness.

    C Programming Lab

    Web Programming Lab

    Semester 2

    Basic Statistics and Probability

    Students gain an understanding of basic statistical concepts, including data collection, classification, and measures of central tendency and dispersion. Emphasis is placed on building analytical skills to interpret data and solve simple statistical and probability problems.

    Data Structures

    Learners are introduced to fundamental data structures such as arrays, linked lists, stacks, and queues along with their operations and applications. The syllabus also covers sorting and searching techniques to organize and retrieve data efficiently. This subject builds core problem-solving and logical skills essential for efficient program design.

    Database Management System

    Designed to build a strong foundation in database systems, this subject explains database concepts, data models, and ER modeling. It covers the relational model, relational algebra, SQL querying, and data manipulation techniques. Learners also understand normalization principles, transaction processing, and advanced SQL features such as cursors and triggers.

    Principle of Programming Languages

    Students explore the principles of programming language design, covering paradigms, compilation, interpretation, and programming environments. The syllabus addresses data types, control flow, subroutines, and abstraction techniques, including object-oriented and functional programming. Learners also study advanced topics such as concurrency, exception handling, and scripting languages like Python and JavaScript.

    Object-Oriented Programming using C++

    Learners study object-oriented programming using C++, exploring classes, objects, and core OOP concepts like inheritance, polymorphism, and encapsulation. The syllabus covers operators, arrays, pointers, constructors/destructors, file handling, and exception management. Students also gain practical experience with C++ standard libraries and modern programming practices.

    Database Management System Lab

    Data Structures using C++ Lab

    Semester 3

    Computer Organization and Architecture

    Students explore computer architecture, including CPU organization, memory, and input/output systems. The syllabus covers micro-operations, instruction cycles, pipelining, vector processing, and advanced I/O mechanisms. Learners also study modern CPU architectures, cache and virtual memory, and the design of contemporary microprocessors like ARM, MIPS, and x86.

    Java Programming

    Learners develop skills in Java programming, focusing on object-oriented principles like classes, objects, inheritance, and abstraction. The syllabus covers arrays, string handling, packages, interfaces, exception handling, and multithreading. Students also gain hands-on experience with GUI development using Swing, event handling, and an introduction to Java frameworks such as Spring.

    Data Communication & Protocols

    Students explore data transmission and communication technologies, including analog and digital signals, transmission media, and encoding techniques. The syllabus covers data link protocols, multiplexing, spread spectrum, and cellular communication across generations from 1G to 6G.

    Operating Systems

    Learners examine operating system concepts, including types, architecture, and process management. The syllabus covers CPU scheduling, synchronization, deadlocks, memory management, and virtual memory. Students also study modern operating systems such as UNIX, Linux, and Windows, with an introduction to cloud-based OS concepts.

    Artificial Intelligence for Problem Solving

    Students explore the fundamentals of artificial intelligence, including intelligent agents, problem-solving, and search techniques. The syllabus covers game playing, knowledge representation, reasoning methods, and planning in AI. Learners are also introduced to uncertain reasoning and the basics of machine learning, including supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

    Java Programming Lab

    Operating System Lab

    Semester 4

    Python Programming

    Learners gain practical skills in Python programming, covering basics, control flow, I/O operations, and core data structures like lists, sets, tuples, and dictionaries. The syllabus includes functions, recursion, object-oriented programming concepts, and use of Python’s standard libraries for text handling, networking, and database access.

    Software Engineering

    Students explore system and software engineering concepts, including system elements, characteristics, and types. The syllabus covers requirement analysis, feasibility studies, software development methodologies, and design tools such as ER diagrams, DFDs, and UML.

    Data Mining & Visualization

    Learners study data mining concepts, including its importance, functionalities, and applications. The syllabus covers operational databases, data warehouses, multidimensional models, and data preprocessing techniques. Students also explore data mining algorithms, classification, clustering, and effective data visualization, including dashboards and advanced charting methods.

    Introduction to Network Security

    Students explore the principles and practices of network security, including goals, trust management, and key security principles. The syllabus covers threats and attacks, access control, cryptography, digital security, and cybersecurity awareness. Learners also study operating system and application security, network protocols, IoT security, web security, and cybercrime prevention techniques.

    Python Programming Lab

    Data Mining and Visualization Lab

    Semester 5

    Core Subjects

    Mobile Application Development

    Learners gain hands-on experience in Android development, exploring the Android platform, architecture, and development environment. The syllabus covers threads, multimedia, user interface design, views and layouts, file management, and SQLite databases. Students also study application components, data handling with Room and LiveData, and practical aspects of building modern Android applications using Kotlin.

    Machine Learning

    Students explore the fundamentals of machine learning, including supervised and unsupervised techniques. The syllabus covers key algorithms such as K-Nearest Neighbors, Naïve Bayes, Decision Trees, Support Vector Machines, clustering methods, and association rule mining.

    Cloud Computing & Applications

    Learners explore cloud computing concepts, architectures, and service models, along with virtualization, containerization, and data center technologies. The syllabus covers cloud security, data management, service management, standards, and interoperability. Students also study cloud migration, emerging trends, and applications, including serverless computing, edge computing, hybrid clouds, and AI/ML integration.

    Machine Learning Lab

    Semester 6

    Core Subjects

    Wireless Communication

    Students study wireless communication systems, covering mobile network evolution, electromagnetic spectrum, antennas, and cellular design concepts. The syllabus includes multiple access and modulation techniques, 2G–5G networks, WLAN, Bluetooth, PANs, wireless sensor networks, satellite communication, and security.

    Unix and Shell Programming

    Learners explore Unix operating system concepts, including its architecture, file system, and command-line operations. The syllabus covers filters, pipes, redirection, processes, Vi editor, and shell scripting from basics to advanced features.

    Big Data

    Learners study Big Data concepts, architecture, storage, and processing using tools like Hadoop and Spark. The syllabus covers data analysis, visualization, cloud integration, security, privacy, and ethics, along with applications in business, healthcare, and social media. Students also explore emerging trends, future opportunities, and practical hands-on setup for Big Data environments.

    Eligibility for online BCA

    Indian students
    NRI & Foreign students

    Educational qualification

    Candidates must have completed 10+2 from a recognized national or state board institution or 10+3 diploma from a recognized national or state institute.

    Grades

    Candidates must have at least 40% marks in aggregate in 10+2 / diploma (35% for reserved categories).

    Educational qualification

    Candidates must have completed 10+2/ intermediate/ senior secondary from a recognized board, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities, in any discipline.

     

    Candidates who have completed their 10+2 or graduate level outside India must produce a certificate of equivalence issued by the Association of Indian Universities.

    Grades

    Candidates must have at least 40% marks in aggregate in 10+2/ intermediate/ senior secondary.

    Other requirements

    Candidates who are not Indian citizens or residing outside India, and holding NRE or PIO status will be billed an international fee for respective degrees & may need to submit documents mentioned but not limited to CV, address proof, valid visa, PR card & passport copy as part of the application process

    What are the eligibility criteria for an Online BCA course? Who can apply?

    Indian Students 

    Educational qualification 

    • Candidates must have completed 10+2 from a recognized national or state board institution or 10+3 diploma from a recognized national or state institute. 

    Grades 

    • Candidates must have at least 40% marks in aggregate in 10+2 / diploma.

    What is the duration of the BCA program?

    Like an on-campus BCA program, the online BCA also is a three-year degree program consisting of six semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 6 years.

    What is the admission process for an online BCA degree?

    Here’s a guide to help you begin your online BCA degree at Manipal University Jaipur:  

    • Step 1: Fill in your basic, education, and work experience-related details and pay the application fee to register.  
    • Step 2: Pay the admission fee for the first semester/year or full program.  
    • Step 3: Upload supporting documents and submit your application.   
    • Step 4: The university will evaluate your documents to confirm your admission.

    What are the specializations available for this course?

    The specializations available for this course are:

    • Data Science & Analytics 
    • Cloud Computing 
    • Cybersecurity 

    Who should opt for online BCA courses?

    Online BCA courses fit the best for fresh graduates who are looking to get a degree in a flexible setting or even for working professionals who are looking to pursue a career in IT.

    What are the documents required for admission into the Bachelor of Computer Application course?

    Mandatory documents: 

    • Proof of identity – Aadhaar card (mandatory for Indian citizens) 
    • Proof of address – Aadhaar card, passport, voter’s ID, gas bill, bank statement, post-paid mobile statement 
    • Class 10 & 12 marksheet 
    • Graduation marksheet (for PG students)

    • CAT / GMAT / MAT valid score for MBA

    • Divyaang certificate

    • Government ID card

    Is work experience mandatory for the BCA program? What qualifies as work experience?

    The online BCA program at the Manipal University Jaipur does not require work experience.

    Are international students eligible to apply for programs at Online Manipal?

    Yes, subject to fulfilling eligibility criteria pertaining to the program you are applying for. Online Manipal accepts applications from both Indian and international students for all programs. Non-Indian applicants should apply via www.onlinemanipal.com/global

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

    • Roll number issued by the university
    • Aadhar number (12 digits)
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@muj.onlinemanipal.com or call +91 7996 600 444 for any queries.

    If I am eligible for a scholarship and pay the entire fee upfront, can I avail both the scholarship and the fee concession (for full payment)?

    Yes. All learners who pay the full program fee upfront can avail 10% fee concession on total program fee upon approval. In addition to the fee concession, if a learner is eligible for a scholarship, he/she can avail the same. The scholarship amount will be calculated on the total program fee.

    How will scholarships be processed for eligible candidates?

    Once a candidate’s scholarship is approved during admission, the scholarship benefit shall be applied as per the below table-

    Periodicity of fee payment Semester 1 Semester 2 Semester 3 Semester 4
    Semester-wise payment     – 1st and 2nd semester scholarship shall be applicable 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Annual payment – – 1st , 2nd and 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Full fee payment at the beginning of the program Scholarship applicable for all semesters of the program shall get refunded.

    For example- In the case of semester-wise fee payment, Semester-1 scholarship benefit shall be applicable in the subsequent semester ie- scholarship benefit of both Semester 1 & 2 shall be applicable while registering for Semester – 2. Thereafter, from Semester – 3, the current semester scholarship benefit shall be applicable.

    If I am funding my fee through a bank loan, can I apply for a fee concession for upfront payment of full program fee?

    • Fee concessions for upfront payment apply only when the program fee is paid by the learner through their own source of funds, such as their own bank account or card payment.
    • Learners who take an independent bank education loan (without subvention or zero-interest EMI) are eligible for this concession.
    • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.

    *EMI amount mentioned above is applicable for the entire program fee and would need to be paid monthly, for the full duration of the program.

    For further details on EMIs and other easy financing options, please contact our admission counsellors.

    Will there be exams for an online BCA program?

    The students are required to take term-end exams, and any other applicable assessments to fulfil the criteria to be eligible for award of degree.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    Students can take up online examinations from any desktop or laptop running a Windows Operating System with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed. The Safe Exam Browser (exam portal) is not compatible with iOS devices.

    Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

    No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

    What is the examination pattern for online degrees at Manipal University Jaipur?

    The University term-end examination (TEE) will be held digitally viz. computer-based and online proctored.

    Candidates can appear for the exams from the comfort of their homes.

    The details of the examination schedule will be made available in the photo admit card. Other details on the examination pattern are available in the program guide provided to students.

    Examination results will be published on the student portal. If a student fails to clear any of the papers (either in TEE or IA or both), he must re-appear by applying to the University through the re-sitting application along with relevant fees, within the due date.

    On satisfying all criteria stipulated by the University, successful students will be awarded their respective program degree by the University.

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both continuous assessments (CA) through assignments and term-end examinations (TEE) which consist of descriptive and multiple-choice questions. A student must score at least 40% in each of these to be declared as having passed the exam. The assessment ratios of TEE to CA is 70:30.

    Practical papers (as applicable)

    The assessment in the practical part is based on performance in guided exercises (i.e., day-to-day exercises) and unguided exercises. The latter will be conducted on the last day of the practical sessions in each subject with an external examiner.

    A student must score at least 40% in both the parts to be declared as having passed.

    What is the minimum grade to complete the online degree program?

    Students must score at least 40% in continuous evaluation and 40% in term-end examinations in each course to successfully complete the program. In case the minimum score is not attained, they will need to apply for a re-sit with applicable fees and appear for particular exam to pass the course.
