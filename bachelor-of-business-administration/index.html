    Semester 1

    Business and Management Functions

    This subject covers essential organizational management functions—planning, organizing, staffing, directing, and controlling. Students learn management evolution, contemporary business issues, and organizational types, developing their strategic thinking, decision-making, and leadership skills for effective management.

    Financial Accounting

    This subject gives a foundation in financial accounting principles and practices, covering how organizations record, summarize, and report financial transactions. Students learn to prepare key financial statements, and develop skills to analyze and interpret financial data for informed decision-making.

    Microeconomics

    This course offers a thorough foundation in microeconomic concepts and their practical use in business contexts. Students will study consumer and firm behavior, analyzing their interactions across different market structures.

    Entrepreneurship and Innovation Management

    This subject explores entrepreneurship evolution, distinguishing entrepreneurs from managers and intrapreneurs while examining decision processes and India's entrepreneurial growth. Students study fundamental theories, and Intellectual Property Rights, including patents, copyrights, and India's legal framework.

    Universal Human Values

    Students learn universal human values—compassion, respect, honesty, integrity, peace, and responsibility—that transcend cultural, religious, and geographical boundaries, forming the foundation for meaningful living and societal well-being. These values guide ethical decision-making, foster harmonious relationships, and promote mutual understanding and respect across humanity.

    Office Automation Tools

    Learners can learn essential Office Automation Tools that streamline administrative and operational tasks in modern workplaces by automating routine activities like document creation, data management, scheduling, communication, and reporting through word processors, spreadsheets, presentation software, email systems, and project management tools.
    Semester 2

    Macroeconomics

    This subject examines aggregate economy functioning, beginning with macroeconomic terminology and data measurement. Students explore key concepts—national income, inflation, unemployment, and economic growth—in both short and long terms, gaining insights into how these factors influence overall economic performance and business operations.

    Financial Management

    Students learn financial management essentials for procuring funds cost-effectively and deploying them efficiently. The course covers key finance manager decisions—financing, investment, dividend, and liquidity, all aimed at maximizing shareholder wealth through the art and science of money management.

    Organizational Behaviour

    Students learn Organizational Behavior essentials for careers in business and industry, understanding the interplay between organizations and individuals. This course examines individual behavior, group dynamics, and organizational aspects, addressing the increasing complexities in modern organizations and preparing students for management roles.

    Community Development

    Students learn community development principles and methods, focusing on community-driven social change through needs assessment, resource mobilization, and long-term planning. The course covers key concepts including social justice, empowerment, capacity building, and civil society's role in fostering diverse and inclusive communities.

    Introduction to Python

    Students learn comprehensive Python programming, covering essential concepts including data types, control structures, functions, object-oriented programming, and file handling. The course introduces popular libraries (Pandas, Matplotlib, NumPy) while emphasizing exception handling and best practices for writing robust code.

    Statistics for Managers

    Students learn statistical tools for business decision-making, covering data collection, organization, and analysis techniques like averages, percentages, and probability. The course develops skills in data interpretation, visual presentations (charts and graphs), and applying statistical methods to solve real-world business problems.

    Business Communication

    Students learn essential business communication skills for organizational effectiveness, understanding how information flows across hierarchical levels for decision-making, direction, and control. The course covers modern communication methods—video conferencing, satellite communication, emails—alongside traditional approaches, developing proficiency in verbal, non-verbal, and digital communication for professional environments.
    Semester 3

    Operations Management

    This course focuses on core operations management principles, examining how organizations create, oversee, and refine production processes for maximum efficiency. Topics include process evaluation, quality control, supply chain coordination, inventory management, and strategic operations, with case studies and real-world examples enabling resource optimization, productivity enhancement, and customer value creation.

    Human Resource Management 

    This course prepares learners for business management careers by exploring how human resources drive organizational success. Organizations survive and thrive through their people's competencies and performance, as human resources make organizations dynamic and serve as prime drivers of all financial and intellectual resources.

    Marketing Management

    This course prepares learners for business careers by covering marketing fundamentals, a key pillar of business. Students understand core concepts including marketing environment and marketing mix elements to effectively bring products and services to market.

    Cost & Management Accounting

    This course covers cost and management accounting principles, focusing on cost measurement, control, and decision-making through costing methods, budgeting, variance analysis, and performance evaluation, equipping learners to support cost efficiency and strategic financial decisions in business environments.

    Interview Skills and Etiquettes  

    This course builds essential interview and professional communication skills, helping learners present themselves with confidence and professionalism. It covers interview formats, effective communication techniques, and workplace etiquette, equipping learners with practical strategies to succeed in diverse interview scenarios and advance their careers.

    Business and Corporate Laws

    Business and Corporate Law introduces students to the legal principles governing business activities and corporate entities. The course covers contracts, sale of goods, partnership and company law, along with intellectual property rights, consumer protection, corporate governance, and emerging areas such as e-commerce laws and corporate social responsibility.

    Business Environment

    This subject examines the business environment and its impact on organizational operations. It explores how government regulations, society, and the global environment influence businesses, while also highlighting how business activities affect society in return.
    Semester 4

    Indian Financial System 

    Learners understand the Indian Financial System and its structure, functions, and operations. It provides foundational knowledge of the evolving financial sector, recent reforms, and ongoing innovations, equipping students with essential financial insights as part of management education.

    Business Research Methods 

    The subject extends foundational research design concepts and focuses on developing practical competencies in qualitative and quantitative research methods. It prepares students to formulate research problems, apply appropriate methodologies, and present research findings effectively.

    Supply Chain Management 

    The subject offers a comprehensive understanding of Supply Chain Management, focusing on the strategic movement of goods and services from suppliers to customers. It covers procurement, inventory, logistics, distribution, demand forecasting, and the role of technology, sustainability, and global trends in modern supply chains.

    E-Commerce 

    This subject explains the relationship between e-business and e-commerce, highlighting how internal digital processes transform into value exchanges across firm boundaries.

    Design Thinking (MLC) 

    This subject introduces a human-centred approach to problem-solving, focusing on empathy, ideation, prototyping, and testing of innovative solutions. Through hands-on projects and collaborative activities, learners develop the confidence to address real-world challenges across business, technology, and social impact domains.

    Environmental Science 

    The subject builds foundational understanding of environmental systems and natural processes that influence human life and communities. It examines the impact of human activities and development on the environment, fostering environmental awareness and responsibility.

    Technology Management

    The subject introduces the concept of technology management and its role in enhancing organizational success and sustainability in today’s corporate environment. It focuses on managerial activities such as planning, organizing, implementing, monitoring, evaluating, and staffing as essential functions for effective organizational performance.
    Semester 5

    Core Subjects

    Fundamentals of Business Analytics

    This subject introduces the fundamentals of business analytics and how data is used to support effective business decisions. Students learn basic data collection, visualization, statistical analysis, and predictive modeling techniques.

    International Business Management 

    Students learn how businesses operate and compete in the global marketplace, focusing on international strategies, cross-cultural management, and global business environments. The subject explores challenges and opportunities of doing business across countries, with insights into global logistics and supply chain management.

    Core Subjects

    Strategic Management

    Learners will develop strategic thinking skills to navigate the dynamic, global business environment. The subject covers strategy formulation and implementation, helping students analyze challenges, align with changing market conditions, and achieve sustainable competitive advantage.

    Management Information System

    Learners will gain a solid understanding of Management Information Systems (MIS) and their role in business decision-making. The subject covers DBMS, ERP, DSS, and e-enterprises, along with decision-making models for areas like finance, accounting, and management, equipping students to plan, develop, and manage information systems effectively.

    Project

    Learners will undertake a six-month project in the 5th semester with a project synopsis and completing a full project report in the 6th semester. Students may choose a project at their workplace or any other relevant location in their chosen elective area. The project work is equivalent to 6 credits and provides practical, hands-on experience to apply their learning.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

    • Roll number issued by the university
    • Aadhar number (12 digits)
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@onlinemanipaladmission.in or call +91-8920 785 477 for any queries.
