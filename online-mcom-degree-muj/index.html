    Online MCom Course curriculum

    Semester 1

    Management Concepts and Organizational Behavior

    Learners will understand fundamental concepts of management and organizational behavior, including the basic theories that explain individual behavior while gaining deeper insight into their own selves. They will also develop an understanding of individual behavior in groups, group and team dynamics, and the fundamentals of behavior in organizations, enabling them to become more effective facilitators and leaders in building high-performing teams.

    Managerial Economics

    Learners will understand how demand and supply function in the economy and develop an understanding of key cost and revenue concepts. They will also explore how different markets operate in the real world, examine the roles of consumption, savings, and investment, and analyze macroeconomic challenges such as inflation, fiscal deficits, and current account deficits.

    Financial Management

    This subject highlights the importance, scope, and relevance of financial management in an organization, along with the fundamental concepts, tools, and techniques required to achieve organizational objectives. They will also learn to apply financial management principles in both theoretical and practical decision-making scenarios and identify financial challenges faced by organizations while proposing appropriate solutions.

    Cost Analysis & Control

    Learners will master the foundational concepts of cost and cost accounting and their essential role in organizational planning and control. Additionally, they will apply advanced cost and management accounting techniques to support managerial decision-making and explore methodologies like TQM and JIT to optimize cost control and management.

    Business and Economic Laws

    Learners will grasp the critical relevance of business laws and essential legal provisions that govern organizations, gaining the ability to interpret and apply legal principles across varied business contexts. They will further hone skills to address everyday operational legal challenges while clearly communicating necessary compliances and formalities throughout different business phases.

    Financial Accounting & Reporting

    Students will gain insight into the fundamentals of financial accounting, encompassing established standards and the comprehensive framework that shapes financial reporting practices. They will acquire practical skills in preparing financial statements, handling consolidation accounting, implementing specific standards in real-world applications, and staying current with evolving trends in financial accounting and reporting.​
    Semester 2

    Research Methodology and Statistical Analysis

    Learners will understand the concept and process of research, including key elements like research design, sampling techniques, data collection, and analysis methods. They will also learn to apply parametric and non-parametric tests for hypothesis testing, along with the process of writing research reports and the importance of ethics in research.

    Management Accounting

    Learners are equipped to effectively apply management accounting techniques in business decision-making, while developing proficiency in analyzing financial statements through essential financial analysis methods. They will also master the principles and real-world applications of marginal costing, alongside awareness of the latest advancements shaping management accounting practices.​

    Marketing Management

    Learners master fundamental marketing concepts, theories, and principles across essential marketing domains, while recognizing marketing's pivotal role as a core organizational policy process. They will also analyze the interplay between marketing decisions and external environmental forces, applying targeted knowledge, concepts, and tools to tackle real-world marketing challenges effectively.​

    Business Environment

    Learners will gain insight into the fundamental concepts of the business environment and its comprehensive framework, encompassing critical elements like political, socio-cultural, legal, and technological factors. They will also develop proficiency in identifying key issues within the business environment and evaluating their strategic implications for organizations.​

    Project Planning, Appraisal & Control

    Learners understand the scope, cost, timing, and quality aspects of projects at all times, maintaining a focus on project success as defined by stakeholders. They will identify project goals, constraints, deliverables, performance criteria, control needs, and resource requirements in consultation with stakeholders, while applying project management practices to launch new programs, initiatives, products, services, and events tailored to stakeholder needs, along with contemporary project management tools and methodologies.

    Management of Financial Institutions, Market and Service

    This subject equips learners to grasp the financial system's pivotal role in fueling economic development, alongside the diverse types of financial markets in India and the core functions of key financial institutions. Learners will also explore a range of financial services while staying updated of emerging trends across financial institutions, markets, and service offerings.​
    Semester 3

    Strategic Management

    Learners will master the core concepts, tools, and principles of business policy and strategic management, while appreciating the strategic management process and the distinctions between strategic analysis, formulation, and implementation. They will also grasp the integration and implementation of strategic plans and policies, considering cultural and ethical factors that influence management, and conduct strategic and competitive analysis using various tools across diverse industries.

    E-commerce

    Learners will understand the concept, relevance, and types of e-commerce, including key models such as B2C, B2B, and C2C. They will also comprehend network and e-commerce infrastructure, e-enterprise management, the process for building an e-commerce presence, and the regulatory framework along with applications and security challenges.

    International Business

    This subject explores the global economic, political, cultural, and social environments shaping firm operations, alongside the international business landscape, its strategies, implications, and management practices. Learners will also assess the global business environment through economic, social, and legal lenses, while mastering core principles of international business and expansion strategies employed by firms worldwide.​

    Corporate Tax Laws and Planning

    This subject deals with the concepts of tax planning, evasion, avoidance, and management, including corporate taxation for non-residents, as well as tax planning strategies for different heads of income. They will also comprehend tax planning for specific management decisions and corporate restructuring scenarios.

    Security Analysis and Portfolio Management

    Learners will explore the fundamental concepts of investment scenarios, financial markets, instruments, and the roles of key market participants such as investors, issuers, and intermediaries. They will also grasp valuation and analysis techniques for financial instruments, theories and tools used in portfolio management, and the essential concepts of portfolio development, management, and evaluation.

    Management Information System

    Learners will understand the role of information technology and decision support systems in businesses, recording current firm issues to solve business problems, while understanding the fundamental principles of computer-based information systems and related techniques. They will also explore various knowledge representation methods and expert system structures to enhance business competitiveness, apply information to assess the impact of the Internet and related technologies on electronic commerce and business, and understand specific threats and vulnerabilities of computer systems.
    Semester 4

    Advanced Corporate Accounting

    Learners get equipped on the preparation of company financial statements along with the valuation of goodwill, shares, and entire businesses, as well as the accounting processes for the issue and redemption of shares. They will also grasp accounting treatments for corporate restructuring and liquidation, human resource accounting, and adjustments for price level changes.

    Audit and Insurance

    Learners will understand the nature, scope, types, and significance of auditing, along with its relationship with other disciplines, including basic concepts of audit planning and programs. They will also grasp internal control, internal check, and related auditing standards, understand secretarial audits for companies and audits of special entities, and the importance of auditing in automated environments.

    Risk Management

    Learners will grasp the core concepts of risk and risk management, along with effective strategies for managing business risks. They will also explore the mechanics and tools of risk management, as well as the relevance of enterprise risk management (ERM) to key stakeholders.

    Business Ethics and Corporate Governance

    This subject covers the fundamentals of business ethics and codes of conduct in business, along with Indian ethos, sanskaras, and self-management practices. Learners will also apply concepts of good corporate governance and investor protection, while dealing with the different laws and practices that underpin corporate governance.

    Indirect Taxes: GST

    Learners will understand the basic concepts of Goods and Services Tax (GST) and other indirect taxes, including the structure of GST, its levy and collection mechanisms, and the system of tax credits. They will also learn about provisions related to registration, exemptions, supply definitions, and filing of returns under the GST Act, along with key aspects of the Customs Act in India.

    Project Report

    Project report writing trains learners to communicate information in an effective and convincing way, thus contributing to the organizational planning and decision making. Learners get to associate themselves with any business organization or can do the study independently.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

    • Roll number issued by the university
    • Aadhar number (12 digits)
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@onlinemanipaladmission.in or call +91-8920 785 477 for any queries.

    How will scholarships be processed for eligible candidates?

    Once a candidate’s scholarship is approved during admission, the scholarship benefit shall be applied as per the below table-

    Periodicity of fee payment Semester 1 Semester 2 Semester 3 Semester 4
    Semester-wise payment     – 1st and 2nd semester scholarship shall be applicable 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Annual payment – – 1st , 2nd and 3rd semester scholarship shall be applicable 4th semester scholarship shall be applicable
    Full fee payment at the beginning of the program Scholarship applicable for all semesters of the program shall get refunded.

    For example- In the case of semester-wise fee payment, Semester-1 scholarship benefit shall be applicable in the subsequent semester ie- scholarship benefit of both Semester 1 & 2 shall be applicable while registering for Semester – 2.     • Learners opting for loans with subvention or zero-interest EMI schemes are not eligible for upfront payment concessions, but they may apply for scholarships if they meet the eligibility criteria.

    Can online degrees from Manipal University Jaipur be recognized in all regions of Canada?

    Yes, Manipal University Jaipur’s degrees are recognized in Canada. Here are the bodies that evaluates Manipal University Jaipur’s degrees for issuing equivalence certificates . These bodies conduct educational credential assessment (ECA) services for both employers and higher education institutions (HEIs).

    World Education Services (WES) – Applicable across US and Canada.
    International Credential Assessment Service of Canada (ICAS)- Applicable across Canada for employment and immigration purposes. ICAS is the only accepted evaluation service for admission to colleges in Ontario province.
    International Credential Evaluation Service (ICES)- The government-mandated ECA service in British Columbia which also serves the interest of all internationally educated applicants throughout Canada.

    Coursera access is valid for one year from the date of activation. Learners are encouraged to make the most of this period to complete courses and certifications.

    No, Coursera access is not included by default. It is an optional add-on that you can choose to purchase after your admission is confirmed.

    You can opt in and activate Coursera access after your admission is approved, either through your student portal or with assistance from your mentor.

    When will I get access to the learning modules?

    Can I get a refund if I choose not to use Coursera after purchasing it?

    No, once purchased, Coursera access is non-refundable and cannot be cancelled, as it provides immediate access to the platform and its content.
