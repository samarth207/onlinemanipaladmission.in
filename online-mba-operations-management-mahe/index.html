The online Master of Business Administration (MBA) in Operations Management from Manipal Academy of Higher Education (MAHE) is designed to equip working professionals with the expertise to scale up their positions in operations across industries. This flexible online MBA in Operations Management covers advanced concepts such as enterprise resource planning, total quality management, supply chain management, project management, services operations management, and operations research to prepare learners for dynamic workplaces. An advanced learning management system allows learners to access reading materials, video tutorials, live and recorded classes, e-libraries, discussion forums, and quizzes from the comfort of their homes. Learners also receive exclusive access to Coursera to boost their career prospects by earning globally relevant certificates from top universities and companies.

    Semester 1

    Managing People & Organizations

    Learners will gain an understanding of individual behavior, group dynamics, organisational structures, and HR practices to manage people effectively in diverse and changing workplace contexts.

    Financial Reporting & Statement Analysis

    Learners will develop the ability to prepare, interpret, and analyse financial statements using accounting frameworks and analytical tools to assess organisational performance, risk, and financial health.

    Managerial Economics

    Learners will use economic concepts to analyze demand, pricing, and market conditions for effective managerial decisions.

    Business Statistics

    Learners will apply statistical concepts and methods, including probability, distributions, sampling, hypothesis testing, and regression to analyse data and support informed business decision-making.

    Business Communication

    Learners will develop effective communication skills for professional settings, covering interpersonal, written, oral, and organisational communication required for business and workplace success.
    Semester 2

    Financial Management

    Learners will apply financial principles to plan, value, and manage funds, investments, risk, and working capital for sound business decisions.

    Marketing Management

    Learners will develop a comprehensive understanding of marketing concepts, consumer behavior, product, pricing, promotion, distribution, and digital and international marketing strategies to drive effective business decisions.

    Strategic Management

    Learners will develop the ability to analyze business environments, formulate and implement strategies, and drive sustainable competitive advantage.

    IT for Business

    Learners will explore how information technology supports business operations, decision-making, strategy, and competitive advantage across modern organizations.

    Business Leadership

    Learners will develop essential leadership skills to guide teams, manage change, and lead ethically in dynamic business environments.

    Research Methodology

    Learners will gain comprehensive knowledge of research methodology, including designing studies, collecting and analyzing data, testing hypotheses, reporting findings, and adhering to ethical research practices.

    How is an MBA for working professionals different from a regular MBA?

    Here’s a quick comparison to help your understanding of the differences between online MBA courses and regular MBA programs: 

    Aspect  MBA for Working Professionals (Online)  Regular MBA 
    Learning Mode  Online, with live + recorded sessions  In-person, campus-based 
    Flexibility  High – Learn anytime, anywhere  Fixed schedule and classroom timings

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email helpdesk@manipalacademyonline.edu.in for any queries.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    The assessment system has been conceived, developed and administered on a rigorous and fair basis to bring out the best in students and prepare them for a successful career.

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both Internal Assessment (IA) and End Term Examination (ETE). The assessment ratio of IA to ETE is 30:70.

    Internal assessment is based on learner’s participation in discussion board, performance in assignments, performance in unit wise graded quizzes and percentage of content access.

    At the end of each semester, the student is required to appear for the End Term Proctored Online Examination.

    A student must score at least 40% in Internal Assessment (IA) to become eligible for End Term Examination (ETE).

    A student must score at least 40% term-end examination to be declared as having passed in a subject.

    What is the grading pattern?

    The university uses 10 Point Grading System – A+ (10), A (9), B (8), C (7), D (6), E (5), F-Fail (0)

    What if I fail to clear some papers/subjects?

    If you fail to clear any paper/subject in End Term Exam (ETE), you must apply for re-sit exams whenever the University conducts by paying a requisite fees/paper.
