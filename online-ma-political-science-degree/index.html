Semester 1

Political Theory

Learners will understand key concepts in political theory, including Marxism, Neo-Marxism, nationalism, multiculturalism, communitarianism, and feminism. The subject also develops the ability to analyze ideas of liberty, equality, justice, and democracy, and engage in discussions on major schools of political thought.

Indian Government and Politics

Learners will understand the roles of the judiciary, the fundamental rights of Indian citizens, and the structure of governance at both state and central levels. The subject also develops the ability to explain and analyze these aspects of the Indian political and legal system.

Major Ideas and Issues in Public Administration

Learners will understand the concept of Public Administration as a discipline and its core principles. The subject also develops the ability to explain approaches, theories, and principles of organization in administrative contexts.

Comparative Politics: Concepts and Methods

Learners will understand Public Administration as a discipline, including its nature and scope. The subject also develops the ability to explain organizational approaches, theories, and principles effectively.

Interpreting Modern India

Learners will understand Public Administration as a discipline and its core principles. The subject also develops the ability to explain organizational approaches, theories, and principles in administrative contexts.
Semester 2

Western Political Thought

Learners will understand the political thoughts of medieval-period leaders and the approaches of theorists like Plato, Aristotle, Machiavelli, Hobbes, and Locke. The subject also develops the ability to compare the ideas of Socialist and Representative thinkers.

International Politics: Theory and Issues

Learners will understand key approaches to International Politics and the global changes in power structures from the World Wars to the Cold War. The subject also develops the ability to analyze issues such as international terrorism and human rights.

Politics in India

Learners will understand different approaches to Indian politics and the role of political parties. The subject also develops the ability to analyze key issues in India, including class, caste, gender, communalism, regionalism, corruption, violence, environmental degradation, illiteracy, and population challenges.

Political Sociology of India

Learners will understand how citizenship is acquired or denied and the role of power and authority in driving social change. The subject also develops the ability to analyze factors and agents that facilitate or hinder nation-building.

Environment & Climate Change

Learners will understand environmental components, processes, and problems arising from human activities and development. The subject also develops skills to identify and solve environmental issues, apply conservation strategies, evaluate business-related environmental concerns, and plan for sustainable development.
Semester 3

Modern Indian Political Thought

Learners will gain an understanding of the ideas of early modernisers, moderates, and extremists, along with alternative trends in Indian thought such as socialist and communist perspectives. The subject also helps students examine the contributions of key thinkers in shaping modern India and its intellectual foundations.

Research Methodology

Learners will understand the foundations of business research, including research methods, design, and their practical relevance in real-world business contexts. They will also develop skills to collect, analyse, and interpret data using statistical tools and software for effective decision-making.

Governance and Public Policy in India

Learners will understand the structure and functioning of Panchayati Raj Institutions and their role in grassroots governance. They will also gain awareness of key democratic and legal frameworks such as the Right to Information Act and the Consumer Protection Act.

Theory and Practice of Democracy

Learners will gain an understanding of the foundations and historical evolution of modern democracy, along with key concepts of citizenship and rights. They will also explore the challenges faced by plural societies in balancing diversity, inclusion, and democratic values.

India’s Foreign Policy

Learners will understand the core principles, goals, and influencing factors of India’s foreign policy. They will also explore policymaking institutions and analyse continuity and change in India’s external relations in the post–Cold War era.
Semester 4

Gender and Politics

Learners will examine the role of gender in politics and understand how feminist perspectives shape political study and research. They will also develop awareness of gender sensitisation and its impact on political systems and practices.

Indo-Pacific Emerging Trends

Learners will understand the geostrategic importance of key regions and critically assess India’s role within these regions and in the wider global context. They will also gain an introductory understanding of maritime security studies and its growing strategic importance.

International Political Economy

Learners will examine the role of gender in politics and understand how feminist perspectives are used in political study and research. The subject also promotes gender sensitisation and highlights its influence on political systems and governance.

Dissertation and Viva Voce

Learners will learn to frame clear research questions and choose suitable research designs and methodologies. They will also develop the skills to plan, execute, and manage an original research project independently.

Learn More About Our MA Program (FAQs)

What are the eligibility criteria for this Online MA in Political Science course? Who can apply?

Candidates must have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree from a recognized university/institution, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities.

What is the duration of the MA in Political Science program?

Like an on-campus MA Political Science program, the online MA also is a two-year degree program consisting of four semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 4 years.

What is the admission process for this online MA in Political Science degree?

Here’s a guide to help you begin your online MA program in Political Science degree at Sikkim Manipal University:

  • Step 2: Pay the admission fee for the first semester/year or full program.

Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

  • Roll number issued by the university
  • Aadhar number (12 digits)
  • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
  • Gender
  • Date of Birth
  • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years.
