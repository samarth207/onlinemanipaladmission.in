The online Master of Arts (MA) in Journalism and Mass Communication from Manipal University Jaipur (MUJ) is designed to produce future-ready media professionals with essential skills that equip them to thrive in the evolving media industry. This flexible two-year online postgraduate degree enables learners to gain hands-on experience in writing for new media, digital publishing, audio-visual editing, script & screenwriting, mobile editing software, and photography. Delivered by experienced faculty, learners in India can master concepts such as news & reporting, broadcast journalism, digital media marketing, and film appreciation - while continuing to full-time work. Learners also receive paid access to Coursera to earn globally recognized certificates from top universities and organizations and 100% placement assistance.

    Online MA Course curriculum

    Explore list of all subjects (semester wise) covered in our Master of Arts in Journalism & Mass Communication (MA-JMC) program

    24 months
    15-20 Hours Per Week
    4 Sem
    84 Credits
    Download Brochure
    Semester 1

    Basics of Language

    This subject introduces learners to the role and significance of language in the media world. It helps them understand how language varies across different media and enables them to use appropriate language styles based on the target audience.

    Social Structure & Current Affairs

    Learners understand the basic framework of social structures and the key factors that shape society. It also builds critical thinking skills to analyze the relationship between social structures, media, and contemporary social issues.

    Fundamentals of Photography

    This subject introduces learners to the fundamental concepts and importance of photography. It helps them understand the aesthetic value of photographs and apply their knowledge to develop creativity, especially in mobile photography.

    Communication Theories and Models

    This subject introduces learners to the origin and evolution of key communication theories and models. It enables them to compare different theoretical paradigms and develop a comprehensive understanding of how various communication theories explain the communication process.

    Concept of News and Reporting

    This subject familiarizes learners with the fundamental aspects of reporting and the evolving nature of news. It helps learners interpret different forms of news writing while developing the ability to analyze ethical practices in the media.

    Development of Media

    Learners understand the role of various media in the process of development. It enables learners to assess the functions and importance of traditional, print, and electronic media, and to analyze their impact on development in both pre and post-independence India.

    Basics of Audio and Visual Communication

    Learners gain an understanding of the fundamental principles of audio and visual communication. The subject explores and compares the skills and techniques used in both media, while enabling learners to apply this knowledge in the creation of meaningful media content.
    Semester 2

    Media Language

    Learners develop a strong foundation in media writing by understanding its basic principles. The subject explores different media languages and enables learners to effectively execute various forms of media writing.

    Political Structure & Current Affairs

    Learners explore the role of politics in shaping society and examine recent political developments. The subject also develops the ability to analyze how political movements contribute to social change.

    Digital Publishing

    This subject introduces learners to the fundamentals of computer software and operating systems, providing a strong foundation in digital technology. It develops essential design knowledge and skills, focusing on visual communication and layout principles. Learners get to design, create, and publish professional-quality brochures, posters, magazines, and newspapers using appropriate software tools, fostering creativity, technical proficiency, and practical application in digital and print media.

    Editing and Layout Designing

    This subject helps learners understand the basic elements and principles of design and their application in page layout. It introduces fundamental editing techniques and provides hands-on experience in designing and publishing brochures, posters, magazines, and newspapers.

    Public Relations

    This subject introduces the role of Public Relations in effective communication. It explores and compares various PR strategies and examines how Public Relations contributes to social change and public awareness.

    Broadcast Journalism

    Learners equip themselves on news production processes and industry practices. It develops employability skills by building practical knowledge of planning and creating effective pre-production strategies for broadcast media.

    Media Laws and Ethics

    This subject introduces learners to various media laws and ethical frameworks governing the media industry. It focuses on analyzing key aspects of media ethics and understanding the practical application of media laws to support responsible professional practice and career development.

    Development Communication

    This subject introduces learners to the principles and concepts of Development Communication and its significance in social transformation. It examines the role of media in rural, urban, and tribal development and builds practical skills in Development Communication for addressing social causes and community empowerment.
    Semester 3

    Writing for New Media

    This subject focuses on online writing techniques and digital storytelling structures used in new media. It explores content creation and promotion strategies across digital platforms and develops the skills required to create effective online content tailored to audience needs and platform requirements.

    Inter Culture Communication and Current Affairs

    This subject introduces the core concepts of Intercultural Communication and examines its relevance in diverse social and professional contexts. It evaluates the effectiveness of different media for intercultural communication tasks, compares key cultural dimensions and communication practices, and develops strategic approaches to address challenges and issues related to intercultural communication.

    Mobile Editing Software

    This subject introduces learners to the features and functionalities of mobile applications for specific tasks. It enables comparison of different apps and provides hands-on experience in creating small projects using audio and video applications.

    Elective Set A: Beats of Journalism

    Learners get introduced to the fundamentals of beats reporting and highlighting the unique demands of different journalistic beats. It enables comparison across various types of beats and provides practical experience in executing a chosen beat as a media professional.

    Elective Set B: Mobile Journalism Advertising Theory & Practice

    This subject introduces learners to the concepts and practices of mobile journalism. It explores the features of technical equipment used in the field and develops practical skills for creating and producing content using mobile devices.

    Communication Research

    This subject explores the relationship between research paradigms, theories, and methodologies. It provides an understanding of different research methods, equipping them with the foundational knowledge to conduct systematic and informed research.

    Minor Specialization Corporate Communication Data Journalism

    Internship

    Learners undergo practical training in media and communication organizations, such as print, radio, television, advertising, or public relations. Evaluation is based on feedback from the host organization and a completion certificate, with assessment conducted at the end of the third semester.
    Semester 4

    Script and Screen Writing

    This subject introduces learners to the core concepts of scriptwriting, emphasizing the differences between audio and visual writing. It develops skills in creating compelling characters and conflicts, and enables learners to write scripts for their own projects.

    Economic Structure and Current Affairs

    Learners get introduced to fundamentals of the Indian economy, exploring the role of government policies and the significance of various economic sectors. It also develops the ability to analyze key economic issues and their impact on society.

    Basics of Audio and Video Editing

    This subject introduces learners to the concepts and techniques of audio and video editing. It provides hands-on experience in editing basic projects and develops an understanding of the aesthetics of audio-visual production.

    Film Appreciation

    This subject explores the historical development of world cinema and fosters critical viewing skills. It examines cinematic techniques, analyzes Indian cinema with a focus on regional films, and develops the ability to produce informed and critical film reviews.

    Dissertation

    The dissertation component equips learners with skills in media research and academic writing. It provides comprehensive knowledge of communication studies and enables learners to conduct independent research using both qualitative and quantitative methods. This is an optional component of the curriculum.

    What are the eligibility criteria for Online MA in Journalism & Mass Communication course? Who can apply?

    Candidates must have completed 10+2+3-year bachelor’s degree from a recognized university/institution, or an equivalent qualification as recognized by the Association of Indian Universities.

    What is the duration of the MA in Journalism & Mass Communication program?

    Like an on-campus MA Journalism & Mass Communication program, the online MA also is a two-year degree program consisting of four semesters. However, students can choose to complete the degree at their own pace in a maximum of 4 years.

    What is the admission process for online MA in Journalism & Mass Communication degree?

    Here’s a guide to help you begin your online MA program in Journalism & Mass Communication degree at Manipal University Jaipur:

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account:

    • Roll number issued by the university
    • Aadhar number (12 digits)
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar)
    • Gender
    • Date of Birth
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar)

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email info@muj.onlinemanipal.com or call +91 7996 600 444 for any queries.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

    No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

    What is the examination pattern for online degrees at Manipal University Jaipur?

    The University term-end examination (TEE) will be held digitally viz. computer-based and online proctored.

    Candidates can appear for the exams from the comfort of their homes.

    The details of the examination schedule will be made available in the photo admit card. Other details on the examination pattern are available in the program guide provided to students.

    Examination results will be published on the student portal. If a student fails to clear any of the papers (either in TEE or IA or both), he must re-appear by applying to the University through the re-sitting application along with relevant fees, within the due date.

    On satisfying all criteria stipulated by the University, successful students will be awarded their respective program degree by the University.

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both continuous assessments (CA) through assignments and term-end examinations (TEE) which consist of descriptive and multiple-choice questions. A student must score at least 40% in each of these to be declared as having passed the exam. The assessment ratios of TEE to CA is 70:30.

    Practical papers (as applicable)

    The assessment in the practical part is based on performance in guided exercises (i.e., day-to-day exercises) and unguided exercises. The latter will be conducted on the last day of the practical sessions in each subject with an external examiner.

    A student must score at least 40% in both the parts to be declared as having passed.

    What is the minimum grade to complete the online degree program?

    Students must score at least 40% in continuous evaluation and 40% in term-end examinations in each course to successfully complete the program. In case the minimum score is not attained, they will need to apply for a re-sit with applicable fees and appear for particular exam to pass the course.
