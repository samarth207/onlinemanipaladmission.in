    Semester 1

    Computational Mathematics

    This subject explores core computational mathematics concepts, covering linear algebra, matrix methods, eigen analysis, graph algorithms, calculus fundamentals, and numerical techniques such as interpolation and integration.

    Probability and Probability Distribution

    This subject introduces the fundamentals of probability theory and probability distributions, and key statistical theorems such as the law of large numbers and central limit theorem.

    Programming with R and Python

    This subject provides hands-on training in R and Python programming, focusing on data manipulation, visualization, exploratory analysis, control structures, and essential libraries like tidyverse, ggplot2, NumPy, and Pandas for practical data-driven applications.

    Statistical Inference

    This subject explores the foundations of statistical inference, covering sampling distributions, estimation methods, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, sample size determination, and non-parametric techniques for drawing reliable conclusions from data.

    Database Management

    This subject provides a comprehensive understanding of database management, covering data modeling, SQL querying, transaction processing, concurrency control, query optimization, and modern concepts like data warehousing and big data systems.
    Semester 2

    Linear Regression Models

    This subject offers an in-depth exploration of linear regression models, focusing on estimation techniques, model interpretation, feature engineering, diagnostic testing, validation methods, and advanced approaches such as regularization, model selection, bootstrapping, and Bayesian regression.

    Time Series Analysis

    This subject explores time series analysis concepts, including trend and seasonal modeling, smoothing techniques, stationarity testing, autocorrelation analysis, and simple forecasting methods using R.

    Applied Multivariate Data Analysis

    This subject explores multivariate data techniques, covering dimensionality reduction, factor and cluster analysis, classification methods, scaling approaches, and advanced tools like conjoint and canonical correlation analysis.

    Machine Learning Methods

    This subject explores core machine learning methods, covering data preparation, model training, feature engineering, supervised and unsupervised learning techniques, clustering approaches, neural network foundations, and key AI applications including computer vision.

    Big Data Analytics

    This subject offers a deep dive into big data technologies, including the Hadoop ecosystem (HDFS, MapReduce, YARN), NoSQL databases under the CAP theorem, Spark’s multi-language architecture, Kafka streaming, and essential management tools like Hive, Pig, and Zookeeper.

    Mini Project

    Mini projects are short, practical assignments that help learners apply specific concepts or tools from a module to real-world scenarios in a focused manner.
    Semester 3

    Design and Analysis of Experiments

    This subject explores the principles of design of experiments, covering randomized and blocked designs, factorial and repeated measures approaches, and advanced layouts like Latin square, split-plot, and crossover experiments for effective data-driven decision-making.

    Stochastic Process

    This subject introduces stochastic processes, focusing on Markov chains, state classification, Poisson processes, and branching models. It also provides a foundation in time series analysis and real-world applications of random process behavior.

    Research Methodology

    This subject provides a comprehensive foundation in research methodology, covering research design, problem formulation, data collection, sampling, measurement, questionnaire development, data analysis techniques, report writing, and ethical considerations in research.

    Deep Learning Principles and Applications

    This subject covers core deep learning concepts, including neural network building blocks, optimization, regularization, and CNN foundations for image classification.

    Categorical Data Analysis and Generalized Linear Models

    This subject provides a comprehensive understanding of categorical data analysis and generalized linear models, GLM components and link functions, logistic regression techniques, model diagnostics, variable selection methods, and extensions to multinomial, ordinal, Poisson, and log-linear regression models.
    Semester 4

    Computer Vision

    This subject introduces computer vision fundamentals, covering image processing, feature extraction, 3D vision, object detection and recognition, deep learning techniques, and real-world applications in healthcare, robotics, and ethical AI.

    Health Technology Assessment

    This subject explores the principles and processes of Health Technology Assessment (HTA), highlighting its role in healthcare decision-making through global frameworks, policy applications, pricing strategies, stakeholder engagement, and the evaluation of clinical and economic evidence.

    Natural Language Processing

    This subject introduces Natural Language Processing fundamentals, including text processing, finite-state methods, n-gram language models, part-of-speech tagging, syntactic structures, and statistical parsing for understanding human language.

    Bayesian Statistical Modelling

    This subject introduces Bayesian statistical modelling, focusing on priors and posterior inference, Bayesian analysis of key distributions, MCMC and Gibbs sampling, hypothesis testing with Bayes factors, regression and survival models, and model evaluation using posterior predictive methods and BIC.

    Programming in SAS for Analytics

    This subject builds practical SAS programming skills for analytics, covering data access and preparation, reporting and export, PROC SQL fundamentals, macros for automation, advanced data manipulation techniques, and hands-on industry-led applications.

    Capstone Project

    By completing a capstone project, learners showcase their ability to independently plan, execute, and deliver a project that reflects industry standards and career readiness.

    Is it mandatory for all online students to have an Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) account?

    Yes. From the academic year 2022-23, it has been made mandatory for all universities offering courses in online or distance learning modes to create ABC accounts for all their students as per the UGC regulation and the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020. 

    Academic Bank of Credits (ABC) is a digital storehouse of the credits earned by students during their academic journey. It is a credit bank for students that allows them to accumulate, verify, transfer, and redeem their credits. This is an initiative from the Ministry of Education, Government of India to enable academic flexibility and students’ mobility across multiple Higher Education Institutes (HEIs).  The University Grants Commission (UGC) has mandated that every learner who is an Indian national needs to submit the following details for creating their ABC account: 

    • Roll number issued by the university 
    • Aadhar number (12 digits) 
    • Name (as mentioned in Aadhaar) 
    • Gender 
    • Date of Birth 
    • Mobile number (linked to their Aadhaar) 

    The university will reach out to enrolled students for details required to create their ABC accounts and will provide these details to the UGC. Once the ABC account is created, the credits earned by the learners in their term-end exam will get deposited into their respective ABC accounts which they can redeem within 7 years. Students can email helpdesk@manipalacademyonline.edu.in for any queries.

    Can students take online examinations from any device?

    Students can take up online examinations from any desktop/laptop with the latest version of the Google Chrome browser installed.

    Is the program fee inclusive of resit and revaluation charges?

    No. Additional charges may be payable for revaluation, resit examinations, duplicate certificates, and other supplementary academic services. These fees are not included in the program fee and are levied as per the respective university’s guidelines and policies.

    What is the evaluation & assessment pattern?

    The assessment system has been conceived, developed and administered on a rigorous and fair basis to bring out the best in students and prepare them for a successful career.

    A student’s performance evaluation will be based on both Internal Assessment (IA) and End Term Examination (ETE). The assessment ratio of IA to ETE is 30:70.

    Internal assessment is based on learner’s participation in discussion board, performance in assignments, performance in unit wise graded quizzes and percentage of content access.

    At the end of each semester, the student is required to appear for the End Term Proctored Online Examination.

    A student must score at least 40% in Internal Assessment (IA) to become eligible for End Term Examination (ETE).

    A student must score at least 40% term-end examination to be declared as having passed in a subject.

    What is the grading pattern?

    The university uses 10 Point Grading System – A+ (10), A (9), B (8), C (7), D (6), E (5), F-Fail (0)

    What if I fail to clear some papers/subjects?

    If you fail to clear any paper/subject in End Term Exam (ETE), you must apply for re-sit exams whenever the University conducts by paying a requisite fees/paper.
